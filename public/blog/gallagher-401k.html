401(k) in Divorce

A 401(k) plan like this one is not divided by the divorce judgment alone. The plan administrator generally needs a separate qualified domestic relations order that clearly identifies the participant, the alternate payee, the plan, and the exact benefit awarded.

Because this is a defined contribution plan, the drafting focus is usually account math and administrative clarity, not benefit commencement rules tied to a pension formula. Clean drafting on valuation dates, gains and losses, and loan allocation helps avoid rejection and post-approval disputes.

QDRO Requirements

  • Correct legal plan name and participant/alternate payee identification.
  • Clear award language using either a percentage or specific dollar amount.
  • A valuation date or valuation period the administrator can apply.
  • Explicit treatment of investment gains and losses after the valuation date.
  • Directions on whether participant loans are included, excluded, or separately addressed.
  • Distribution language that matches plan procedures for a separate account, rollover, or direct payment.

Division Formulas

Most Gallagher 401(k) drafts will use one of two structures:

  • Percentage award: useful when the parties want the alternate payee to receive a set share of the vested account balance as of a stated date, adjusted for gains and losses afterward.
  • Fixed dollar award: useful when the settlement sets a negotiated amount that must be segregated and adjusted according to plan-acceptable earnings language.

The better formula depends on the settlement terms, market volatility, and whether contributions, withdrawals, or loans changed the account during the relevant period.

Steps

  1. Confirm the exact plan name and current recordkeeper contact.
  2. Request the plan's written QDRO procedures and any sample order.
  3. Collect the latest account statement and determine the agreed award structure.
  4. Draft the order with valuation, gains/losses, and loan language tailored to the plan.
  5. Seek pre-approval review if the administrator allows it.
  6. Obtain court signature and submit the entered order for qualification.
  7. Track establishment of the alternate payee account and rollover or distribution paperwork.

Common Mistakes

  • Using an abbreviated or outdated plan name.
  • Failing to state whether gains and losses apply after the valuation date.
  • Ignoring outstanding plan loans or treating them inconsistently with the settlement.
  • Assuming public examples are enough without confirming current administrator procedures.
  • Submitting a signed order before checking whether pre-approval review is available.
  • Leaving rollover and payment language too vague for recordkeeping staff to process.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a QDRO divide the Arthur J. Gallagher & Co. Employees Savings & Thrift Plan?

Yes. As a 401(k) defined contribution plan, it is typically divided by a qualified domestic relations order that specifies the awarded share and the administrative terms needed for processing.

How is the alternate payee's share usually handled?

Available public plan summaries indicate the alternate payee may receive a separate account after the order is approved, which is common for 401(k) plans.

Can the alternate payee receive funds right away?

Public references suggest an immediate distribution or direct rollover may be available after qualification and setup of the separate account, subject to plan procedures and tax considerations.

What should you do if public plan documents are hard to find?

Request the current summary plan description, QDRO procedures, sample language if available, and a current administrator or recordkeeper contact directly from the plan.

Why does plan-specific drafting matter here?

Because thin public documentation increases the risk of avoidable rejection. The order should match current plan procedures on valuation, gains and losses, loan handling, and post-approval distribution steps.
