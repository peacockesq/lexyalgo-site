401(k) in Divorce

The Prologis 401(k) Savings Plan is a defined contribution plan, which means the participant holds an individual account rather than a future pension promise. In divorce, that account — or the portion of it attributable to the marriage — may be divisible through a Qualified Domestic Relations Order. The division can be expressed as a percentage, a fixed dollar amount, or another formula tied to a specific valuation date.

Because defined contribution accounts move with investment performance, the order must address what happens to the assigned share between the date used for calculation and the date the alternate payee's money is actually separated. Ignoring gains and losses can create disputes after entry. The Prologis plan permits both pre-tax and Roth contributions, so if the participant has both source types on record, the QDRO should specify whether each is divided pro rata or by another agreed method.

Prologis also provides a 50% employer match on contributions up to 12% of eligible compensation. Match money may be subject to a vesting schedule. An alternate payee may be entitled to a share of unvested employer contributions depending on the settlement and the plan's vesting rules — this detail should be addressed explicitly in the order or confirmed with the plan administrator.

QDRO Requirements

A domestic relations order submitted for the Prologis 401(k) Savings Plan must comply with ERISA, the Internal Revenue Code, and the plan's own qualification procedures. Fidelity administers QDRO review for this plan. At minimum, the order should:

  • Identify the correct plan by name: Prologis 401(k) Savings Plan.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with sufficient specificity for administrator review.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula used to determine the alternate payee's share.
  • Specify the valuation date or triggering event used for calculation.
  • State whether the assigned share is adjusted for investment gains and losses from the valuation date through segregation.
  • Address the treatment of pre-tax and Roth balances separately if both are present in the account.
  • Address any outstanding loan balance if one exists at the time the order is prepared.
  • Not require any form or benefit not otherwise available under the plan.

Division Formulas

Percentage formula

A percentage award assigns the alternate payee a stated share of the participant's vested account balance as of a specific date. Example: the alternate payee receives 50% of the participant's vested account balance as of the date of separation, adjusted for investment gains and losses until segregation. This is the most common approach for 401(k) plans with ongoing market exposure.

Fixed dollar formula

A fixed dollar amount can work when the settlement is structured around a specific number. If this method is used, the order should still address whether that dollar amount is adjusted for earnings or losses after the valuation date. Leaving this open can create implementation problems.

Pre-tax and Roth treatment

If the participant account holds both pre-tax and Roth balances, the QDRO should specify how each source is handled. A pro rata division across all sources is often the cleanest approach. Alternatively, the order can specify one source to be divided first. The key is leaving no ambiguity for the plan administrator to resolve on its own.

Gains and losses

The Prologis plan invests in a range of assets including Prologis common stock. From the valuation date to the date of actual segregation, the account value may change. The order should clearly state whether the alternate payee's share tracks that investment experience or is fixed at the valuation date amount.

Steps to Divide the Plan

  1. Confirm the exact plan name from the participant's most recent account statement: Prologis 401(k) Savings Plan.
  2. Gather the participant's current account information — total balance, pre-tax balance, Roth balance, loan balance (if any), and any unvested employer match.
  3. Decide on the division formula: percentage, fixed dollar, or another clearly stated method with gains and losses treatment.
  4. Draft the QDRO using language consistent with Fidelity's plan-specific QDRO guidelines. Use qdro.fidelity.com for plan-specific approval requirements.
  5. Submit the draft for pre-review through Fidelity's QDRO Center before court entry to catch drafting issues early.
  6. Obtain court entry of the signed domestic relations order.
  7. Submit the certified, court-entered order to Fidelity with any required paperwork and follow through to confirmation of qualification and account segregation.

Common Mistakes

  • Leaving gains and losses unstated: in a moving investment account, undefined language creates disputes. The order should say exactly what happens between valuation date and segregation.
  • Ignoring Roth balances: if Roth money exists in the account, the order should address it explicitly rather than leaving it for the administrator to infer.
  • Not addressing outstanding loans: if the participant has an outstanding plan loan, the order must address whether the alternate payee's share is based on the gross balance or net of the loan.
  • Vesting blindspot: employer match contributions may not be fully vested. An order that assumes full vesting may result in an award smaller than expected.
  • Using a generic template: Fidelity administers hundreds of employer plans, each with plan-specific requirements. A generic 401(k) order may not satisfy Prologis-specific procedures. Use Fidelity's plan-specific review channel.

FAQ

Does the Prologis 401(k) Savings Plan require a QDRO for divorce division?

Yes. The plan is an ERISA-governed defined contribution plan. A divorce decree alone is not sufficient to divide the account. The plan requires a qualifying domestic relations order reviewed and approved by the plan administrator before any distribution to an alternate payee.

Who processes QDROs for the Prologis plan?

Fidelity Investments serves as the plan's trustee and recordkeeper and handles QDRO review. The Fidelity QDRO Center at qdro.fidelity.com provides plan-specific guidelines and a model order drafting tool. The Fidelity Benefits Service Center can also be reached at 1-800-343-0860.

Can both pre-tax and Roth balances be divided?

Yes, to the extent both exist in the participant's account. The QDRO should address each source type explicitly — a pro rata division across both sources is often the cleanest approach, but the settlement terms control what actually goes into the order.

Does Prologis have an employer match, and does it affect the QDRO?

Yes. Prologis matches 50% of participant contributions, up to 12% of eligible compensation. Employer contributions may be subject to a vesting schedule. An alternate payee may be entitled to a share of unvested employer contributions depending on the settlement and vesting rules. Confirm current vesting terms before drafting.

Where can I get Prologis's current QDRO procedures?

Use the Fidelity QDRO Center at qdro.fidelity.com. That portal provides plan-specific approval guidelines for plans Fidelity administers, including the Prologis 401(k) Savings Plan. No official public model QDRO for this plan was found through standard research channels.
