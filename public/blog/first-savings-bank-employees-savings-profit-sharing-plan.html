401(k) in Divorce

The First Savings Bank plan is a defined contribution account, which means the participant holds an individual balance rather than a monthly pension formula. In divorce, that usually means the alternate payee is awarded either a percentage of the vested account balance or a fixed dollar amount as of a specified valuation date. The challenge is specifying the award precisely enough that the plan administrator can implement it without ambiguity.

This plan includes a profit sharing component alongside standard 401(k) salary deferrals, and it permits Roth contributions. If the participant account holds both pre-tax and Roth money, the order needs to address each source — otherwise the administrator is left guessing how to split them. An outstanding participant loan on the account creates the same kind of ambiguity, and the order should answer it directly.

QDRO Requirements

A qualifying domestic relations order for this plan must meet ERISA's baseline content requirements and Pentegra's administrative procedures. At minimum, the order should:

  • Name the plan exactly: FIRST SAVINGS BANK EMPLOYEES' SAVINGS & PROFIT SHARING PLAN.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee clearly enough for administrator review.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula used to determine the alternate payee's share.
  • Specify the valuation date or triggering event for the calculation.
  • Address investment gains and losses from the valuation date through segregation or distribution.
  • Address pre-tax and Roth source treatment if both exist in the account.
  • Address loan balance treatment if an outstanding loan is on the participant's account.
  • Avoid requiring any benefit form or distribution option not available under the plan.

Division Formulas

Percentage of account balance

The alternate payee receives a stated percentage of the participant's vested account balance as of the valuation date, adjusted for investment gains and losses until segregation. This is the most common approach for defined contribution plans.

Fixed dollar amount

The alternate payee receives a specific dollar amount. This can be cleaner when the settlement uses a hard number, but the order should still address whether that amount adjusts for investment gains and losses between the valuation date and segregation.

Pre-tax and Roth sources

If the participant holds both pre-tax and Roth money, specify how each source is allocated. The most straightforward approach is a pro rata division: the alternate payee receives the same percentage of each source as they receive of the total balance.

Loan balance treatment

If the participant has an outstanding 401(k) loan, state whether the alternate payee's share is calculated from the gross balance before subtracting the loan, or from the net balance after. Different approaches affect how much the alternate payee actually receives.

Common Mistakes

  • Wrong plan name: using a shortened or informal name instead of the exact Form 5500 plan name.
  • No gains and losses language: silence on investment experience leaves the award value uncertain between the valuation date and segregation.
  • Ignoring Roth balances: if the participant has made Roth contributions, the order should say how that source is divided to avoid implementation questions.
  • Overlooking the loan: an outstanding 401(k) loan affects the economics of the award; the order should address it.
  • Skipping pre-review: when no official public model order is available, sending a draft to the plan administrator before court entry avoids rejection and amendment costs.

FAQ

Does dividing the First Savings Bank plan in divorce require a QDRO?

Yes. A divorce decree alone cannot divide an employer-sponsored ERISA plan. A qualified domestic relations order satisfying federal requirements and the plan's review procedures is required before benefits can be paid to an alternate payee.

Who processes QDROs for this plan?

Pentegra Services Inc. is identified as plan administrator on the Form 5500 filing. Contact Pentegra directly for QDRO submission procedures. No official public model order was located in the research used for this page.

Should the order address both Roth and pre-tax balances?

Yes, if both exist. State clearly how each source is divided — typically a pro rata allocation in the same percentage as the total award is the cleanest approach.

Why does gains and losses language matter?

Because this is a participant-directed investment account. The balance changes between the valuation date and the date the alternate payee's account is established. Specifying whether the assigned amount tracks investment performance prevents disputes over timing differences.
