pSemi 401(k) in Divorce

The pSemi Corporation 401(k) Plan is a defined contribution plan, meaning participants hold individual investment accounts rather than a pension formula. The account balance grows or shrinks with market performance over time, which is why timing matters in divorce division — the value at the date selected for the QDRO calculation may differ from the value when the alternate payee's share is actually segregated and distributed.

pSemi's plan includes both an employer match component and a profit-sharing component based on company earnings. Both types of employer contributions may be subject to vesting schedules, meaning the participant's right to those funds may depend on years of service. If there is unvested employer money in the account at the time of the QDRO, the order should address whether the alternate payee shares in vested funds only, or whether unvested contributions are included if and when they vest.

QDRO Requirements

A qualified domestic relations order for the pSemi Corporation 401(k) Plan must satisfy ERISA and the Internal Revenue Code, and pass the plan administrator's own review process. A well-drafted order should:

  • Identify the plan by its correct name: PSEMI CORPORATION 401(K) PLAN.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee clearly.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula for the alternate payee's share.
  • Specify the valuation date or triggering event used for the calculation.
  • State whether the alternate payee's share is adjusted for investment gains and losses after the valuation date through segregation or distribution.
  • Address pre-tax and Roth balances separately if both exist in the participant's account.
  • Address any outstanding plan loan balance if applicable.
  • Avoid requiring any benefit or form of payment not available under the plan.
Practice point: No official public model QDRO was found for this plan. Submitting a draft to the pSemi plan administrator for pre-approval before court entry reduces the risk of rejection and post-entry amendments.

Division Formulas

Percentage of account balance

The most common approach for a defined contribution plan. The alternate payee receives a stated percentage of the participant's vested account balance as of a specified date, adjusted for subsequent investment gains and losses until the share is segregated.

Fixed dollar amount

The alternate payee receives a specific dollar figure. If this method is used, the order should address whether the amount is adjusted for earnings between the calculation date and the date of distribution, or whether the award is a frozen number.

Gains and losses language

Because the account is invested in market instruments, the alternate payee's share will differ from the valuation-date figure if the money stays in the plan while the QDRO is processed. Stating clearly whether gains and losses apply avoids disputes during implementation.

Vesting considerations

The plan includes employer match and profit-sharing contributions that may vest over time. If the participant has unvested employer funds, the order should address whether the alternate payee's share is limited to vested funds or also captures unvested amounts if and when they vest.

Steps to Divide the pSemi 401(k)

  1. Obtain a current account statement showing the participant's total balance, source breakdown (pre-tax, Roth, employer contributions), and any outstanding loan balance.
  2. Confirm the plan name as PSEMI CORPORATION 401(K) PLAN and the plan administrator contact through pSemi HR.
  3. Choose the division method: percentage of balance, fixed dollar amount, or another clearly stated formula.
  4. Address gains and losses language consistent with the settlement terms.
  5. If the account includes Roth and pre-tax sources, specify how each is divided.
  6. Submit a draft order to pSemi HR or the plan's designated QDRO review contact for pre-approval.
  7. After court entry, submit the certified signed order and follow through until the plan confirms qualification and the alternate payee account is established.

Common Mistakes

  • Skipping gains and losses language: in a defined contribution plan, the account balance can move significantly while the QDRO is processed. Silence on this point creates avoidable disputes.
  • Ignoring the profit-sharing component: pSemi's plan includes both a match and profit-sharing contributions. A QDRO that only addresses elective deferrals may leave a portion of the marital estate unaddressed.
  • Using a generic order: boilerplate 401(k) language may not map correctly to pSemi's specific plan structure.
  • Skipping pre-approval: when no public model QDRO exists, a draft pre-review with the plan administrator catches problems before court entry.
  • Not checking for outstanding loans: an unresolved plan loan can complicate distribution timing and amount.

FAQ

Does the pSemi Corporation 401(k) Plan require a QDRO for divorce division?

Yes. A divorce decree alone does not divide an employer-sponsored 401(k). The pSemi plan requires a qualified domestic relations order before it can distribute any benefit to an alternate payee.

What type of plan is the pSemi Corporation 401(k)?

It is a defined contribution profit-sharing plan. Participants have individual accounts rather than a pension annuity, and account balances change with investment performance.

Does pSemi's plan include an employer match?

Yes. pSemi's official careers page confirms an employer match. The specific matching formula and any profit-sharing contribution schedule are not publicly disclosed — confirm with pSemi HR.

Why does gains and losses language matter in this QDRO?

Because the account is invested in market instruments, its value can rise or fall between the valuation date and the date the alternate payee's share is distributed. Clear language on this point prevents disputes over whether the alternate payee shares in those changes.
