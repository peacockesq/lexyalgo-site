401(k) in Divorce

The RevSpring 401(k) is a defined contribution plan, which means the participant holds an individual account balance rather than a pension formula. That structure allows for fairly direct division — the alternate payee is typically awarded a percentage or a fixed dollar amount of the account balance as of a specific date. The challenge is specifying the award precisely enough that the plan administrator can implement it without ambiguity.

RevSpring's plan includes a profit sharing component alongside standard 401(k) salary deferrals. If the participant account holds both pre-tax and Roth money, the order needs to address each source — otherwise the plan administrator is left guessing how to split them. Similarly, an outstanding participant loan on the account creates a question the order should answer: does the alternate payee's share get calculated before or after subtracting the loan balance?

QDRO Requirements

A qualifying domestic relations order for the RevSpring 401(k) Plan must meet ERISA's minimum content requirements and the plan's specific procedures. At minimum, the order should:

  • Name the plan exactly: RevSpring, Inc. 401(k) Plan.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with sufficient specificity for administrator review.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula used to determine the alternate payee's share.
  • Specify the valuation date or triggering event for the calculation.
  • Address investment gains and losses from the valuation date through segregation or distribution.
  • Address pre-tax and Roth source treatment if both exist in the account.
  • Address loan balance treatment if an outstanding loan is reflected on the participant's account.
  • Avoid requiring any benefit or form of distribution not available under the plan.

Division Formulas

Percentage of account balance

The alternate payee receives a stated percentage of the participant's vested account balance as of the valuation date, adjusted for investment gains and losses until segregation. This is the most common approach for 401(k) plans and gives the alternate payee a proportional share of market performance after the division date.

Fixed dollar amount

The alternate payee receives a specific dollar amount. This can be cleaner when the settlement uses a hard number, but the order should still address whether that amount adjusts for gains and losses. Silence on that point can create disputes later.

Pre-tax and Roth sources

If the participant holds both pre-tax and Roth money, specify the allocation across sources. The most straightforward approach is a pro rata division: the alternate payee receives the same percentage of each source that they receive of the total. This avoids ambiguity about which dollars are being transferred.

Loan balance treatment

If the participant has an outstanding 401(k) loan, state whether the alternate payee's share is calculated from the gross account balance before subtracting the loan, or from the net balance after. Different approaches affect how much the alternate payee receives, so the order should be explicit.

Steps to Divide

  1. Confirm the exact plan name from the participant's most recent benefit statement: RevSpring, Inc. 401(k) Plan.
  2. Obtain the participant's current account statement showing pre-tax balance, any Roth balance, and any outstanding loan balance.
  3. Confirm the current recordkeeper and QDRO review procedures — contact RevSpring HR or check the Fidelity QDRO Center at qdro.fidelity.com if Fidelity is confirmed as the recordkeeper.
  4. Choose the division method: percentage, fixed dollar, or another clearly stated formula.
  5. Draft the order with explicit gains/losses, source treatment, and loan treatment language.
  6. Submit for plan administrator pre-review before court entry when possible.
  7. After court entry, submit the certified signed order with any required accompanying documents and follow through until the alternate payee's account is fully established.

Common Mistakes

  • Wrong plan name: using a shortened or informal plan name instead of the exact plan name from Form 5500 or the participant's statement.
  • No gains and losses language: for a participant-directed account, silence on investment experience leaves the award value uncertain between the valuation date and segregation.
  • Ignoring Roth balances: if the participant has contributed Roth dollars, the order should say how that source is divided. The tax treatment of Roth and pre-tax money differs, and the plan administrator needs clear direction.
  • Overlooking the loan: if the participant has an outstanding 401(k) loan, failing to address how it affects the alternate payee's share can produce a result neither party intended.
  • Skipping pre-review: when no official public model order is available, sending a draft to the plan administrator before court entry helps avoid rejection and amendment costs afterward.

FAQ

Does dividing the RevSpring 401(k) in divorce require a QDRO?

Yes. A divorce decree alone cannot divide an employer-sponsored 401(k) plan. A qualifying domestic relations order that satisfies federal requirements and the plan's review procedures is required before any benefits can be paid to an alternate payee.

Who processes QDROs for the RevSpring 401(k)?

Third-party sources report Fidelity Investments as the recordkeeper. If confirmed, Fidelity's QDRO Center provides model orders and review guidelines for the plan. Confirm the current recordkeeper directly with RevSpring before submitting.

Should the order address both Roth and pre-tax balances?

Yes, if both exist. State clearly how each source is divided — typically a pro rata allocation in the same percentage as the total award is the cleanest approach.

Why does gains and losses language matter?

Because this is a participant-directed investment account. The balance can change between the valuation date and the date the alternate payee's account is actually established. Specifying whether the assigned amount tracks investment performance prevents disputes over timing differences.

Where can I get the current QDRO procedures for this plan?

Start with the Fidelity QDRO Center (qdro.fidelity.com) if Fidelity is confirmed as the recordkeeper, or contact RevSpring's HR department directly. No official plan-issued model QDRO was identified in the research used for this page.
