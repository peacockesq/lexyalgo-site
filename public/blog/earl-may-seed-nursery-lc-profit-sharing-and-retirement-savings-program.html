401(k) and Profit Sharing in Divorce

The Earl May Seed & Nursery LC program is a defined contribution plan that includes both employee salary deferrals and employer profit sharing contributions. In divorce, the account is divided at the individual account level: the alternate payee is typically awarded a percentage of the vested balance or a fixed dollar amount as of a specific valuation date.

The employer match and profit sharing components are generally held in the same participant account and divided along with the rest of the balance. If any employer contributions are not yet vested at the valuation date, the order should address whether unvested amounts are included or excluded from the award. An outstanding participant loan should be explicitly addressed too — the choice between a gross-balance or net-balance calculation affects the size of the alternate payee's share.

QDRO Requirements

A qualifying domestic relations order for this plan must satisfy ERISA's baseline requirements and the plan administrator's review procedures. At minimum, the order should:

  • Name the plan exactly: EARL MAY SEED & NURSERY LC PROFIT SHARING AND RETIREMENT SAVINGS PROGRAM.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee clearly enough for administrator review.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula used to determine the alternate payee's share.
  • Specify the valuation date or event controlling the calculation.
  • Address gains and losses from the valuation date through segregation or distribution.
  • Address vesting of employer contributions if any are unvested at the time of division.
  • Address participant loan treatment if a loan exists.
  • Avoid requiring any benefit form or option the plan does not provide.

Division Formulas

Percentage of vested account

The alternate payee receives a stated percentage of the participant's vested account balance as of the valuation date, adjusted for gains and losses through segregation. All vested sources — employee deferrals, employer match, and profit sharing — are typically included in the denominator.

Fixed dollar amount

The alternate payee receives a specific dollar figure. The order should address whether that amount adjusts for investment gains and losses between the valuation date and implementation.

Employer contributions and vesting

If profit sharing or matching contributions are not fully vested at the valuation date, the order should address whether unvested amounts are included in the award, excluded entirely, or subject to vesting contingencies.

Loan balance treatment

If there is an outstanding participant loan, state whether the award is calculated from the gross balance before the loan or the net balance after. That choice directly affects the alternate payee's share.

FAQ

Does dividing this plan in divorce require a QDRO?

Yes. A divorce decree alone cannot divide an ERISA profit sharing or 401(k) plan. A qualified domestic relations order is required before any alternate payee distribution.

Who processes QDROs for this plan?

The Finway Group is noted as the administrator. Third-party sources suggest a Great-West / Empower legacy recordkeeping relationship. Confirm current procedures with The Finway Group before drafting or filing.

Does the employer match get included in the division?

Generally yes, to the extent it is vested. A percentage-of-vested-account award includes all fully vested sources. If any employer contributions are unvested, the order should address their treatment explicitly.

How do gains and losses work after the valuation date?

This is a participant-directed plan, so the balance moves with investment performance. The order should specify whether the alternate payee's share adjusts for those changes through segregation.

Where can I get current QDRO procedures for this plan?

Contact The Finway Group or Earl May Seed & Nursery LC directly. No official model QDRO or public plan-specific procedures were identified in the research used for this page.
