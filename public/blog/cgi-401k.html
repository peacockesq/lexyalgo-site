401(k) in Divorce

The CGI plan is a defined contribution plan, so the divorce issue is usually how to divide an account balance rather than how to value a future monthly pension benefit. That still does not make it automatic. A plan administrator needs a domestic relations order that can be applied to the account without guesswork.

For this plan, the research points to three drafting pressure points. First, the SPD confirms both pre-tax and Roth 401(k) contributions, so the order should make clear whether the alternate payee receives a proportional share of each source or some other specified allocation. Second, the SPD includes loan provisions, which means the order should say whether the division is based on a gross or net balance if a loan is outstanding. Third, because this is an investment-based account, the order should specify the valuation date and whether later gains or losses apply.

Even when the settlement sounds simple, vague language can create avoidable disputes at implementation. A clean CGI 401(k) QDRO should translate the settlement into directions the service provider can actually administer.

QDRO Requirements

A workable order for this plan should identify the participant and alternate payee, define the awarded share with precision, and match the plan's qualification process. The official SPD is especially useful here because it confirms that written QDRO procedures exist and are available for free from the service provider.

  • State the award as a fixed dollar amount, percentage, or another defined formula tied to a specific valuation date.
  • Say whether post-valuation investment gains and losses apply through segregation, transfer, or distribution.
  • Address pre-tax and Roth balances directly so the tax character of the award is intentional rather than accidental.
  • Clarify whether employer matching or other vested employer sources are included in the assigned share.
  • Specify how any outstanding participant loan is handled in the calculation.
  • Check the current plan procedures before final submission instead of assuming another 401(k) plan's review standards will carry over.

Best official source: The CGI SPD expressly says the administrator has QDRO qualification procedures and that you or your beneficiary may obtain a copy without charge from the service provider. That is stronger and more useful than relying on generic third-party summaries alone.

Division Formulas

The right formula depends on the settlement, but the order should still be explicit about account sources, loan treatment, and post-valuation adjustments. For a plan with Roth and pre-tax money, source-level clarity matters.

  • Fixed percentage as of a stated date: useful when the alternate payee is receiving a share of the account, usually with gains and losses applied after the valuation date until segregation.
  • Fixed dollar amount: workable when the settlement sets a specific sum, but the order should still address earnings or losses between the valuation date and actual transfer.
  • Pro rata source allocation: often the cleanest approach when the intent is for the alternate payee to receive the same relative mix of pre-tax, Roth, and vested employer amounts held in the participant's account.

What should not be left open is whether the award is calculated before or after a participant loan, whether Roth money is included, or whether later market movement follows the assigned share. That kind of silence creates review delays and post-approval arguments.

Steps

  1. Collect current account statements and identify all money sources, including pre-tax, Roth, employer match, rollovers, and any outstanding loan balance.
  2. Request the current CGI QDRO procedures from the service provider, using the SPD's instruction that they are available without charge.
  3. Confirm the settlement terms, especially the valuation date, gains and losses treatment, and whether the award is source-proportional.
  4. Draft the order with direct language covering source buckets, vested employer money, and loan treatment.
  5. Use any available pre-approval or informal review process before presenting the order for signature.
  6. After qualification, confirm whether the alternate payee will receive a separate account, direct rollover, or other available payout option under the current procedures.

Common Mistakes

  • Leaving out the valuation date or defining it in a way the administrator cannot apply.
  • Failing to state whether post-valuation gains and losses follow the awarded share.
  • Ignoring the difference between Roth and pre-tax money.
  • Not addressing whether employer matching contributions are included.
  • Using an account balance without explaining how an outstanding loan affects the calculation.
  • Assuming a third-party website is the plan's official procedure when the SPD confirms that actual QDRO procedures are available directly from the service provider.

FAQ

Does the CGI Technologies and Solutions Inc. 401(k) Savings Plan require a QDRO for divorce division?

Usually yes. A divorce decree by itself typically does not authorize the plan to transfer part of a participant's 401(k) account to an alternate payee.

Why should the order address Roth and pre-tax money separately?

Because the official SPD says participants may make both pre-tax and Roth 401(k) contributions. Clear source language helps preserve the intended tax treatment of the award.

How should an outstanding participant loan be handled?

The order should explain whether the awarded share is calculated using an account balance that includes or excludes the loan so the administrator is not left to decide the math.

Can the alternate payee receive a separate account or rollover after approval?

Available secondary research says that is typically possible, but the actual options should be confirmed through the plan's current procedures at the time the order is reviewed.

Where do the parties get the plan's QDRO procedures?

The SPD says the procedures may be obtained without charge from the service provider, which is exactly where counsel should start before finalizing the order.
