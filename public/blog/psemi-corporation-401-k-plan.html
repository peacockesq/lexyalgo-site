In Divorce

The PSEMI CORPORATION 401(K) PLAN is a defined contribution plan, so division in divorce usually comes down to an account-balance award rather than a pension formula. That means the main drafting work is precision: exact plan identification, valuation date, gains and losses, and any loan or source-level issues that could change the actual dollars transferred.

Public detail on this plan is limited, which makes administrator confirmation more important than usual. Before the order goes to court, confirm the current recordkeeper, whether the account includes Roth or other separate sources, and whether a participant loan is outstanding.

QDRO Requirements

A qualifying domestic relations order for the PSEMI CORPORATION 401(K) PLAN should be drafted to satisfy ERISA and the plan administrator's current review procedures. At minimum, the order should:

  • Name the plan exactly as shown in the plan documents and Form 5500.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with enough detail for administrator review.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula for the alternate payee award.
  • Specify the valuation date or event used for the calculation.
  • Address investment gains and losses from the valuation date through segregation or distribution.
  • Address separate contribution sources such as pre-tax, Roth, rollover, profit-sharing, or stock components if present.
  • Address participant loan treatment if a loan exists on the account.
  • Avoid awarding any benefit or form of payment the plan does not provide.
  • No public official QDRO procedure packet was located, so administrator pre-review is the smart play.

Division Formulas

Percentage of account balance

The alternate payee receives a stated percentage of the vested account as of the valuation date, adjusted for gains and losses until segregation. This is usually the cleanest approach for a 401(k)-type plan.

Fixed dollar amount

The alternate payee receives a stated dollar amount. If you use this approach, say explicitly whether that amount is adjusted for gains and losses after the valuation date.

Source-by-source allocation

If the account includes multiple sources such as pre-tax, Roth, rollover, profit-sharing, or employer stock, the order should say whether the award is divided pro rata across each source or by another stated method.

Loan treatment

If the participant has an outstanding plan loan, the order should specify whether the alternate payee share is calculated from the gross balance, the net balance, or another agreed figure.

Steps to Divide

  1. Confirm the exact plan name from the participant's most recent statement or plan materials.
  2. Get the current account statement showing balance, contribution sources, and any outstanding loan.
  3. Choose the division formula: percentage award, fixed dollar award, or another clear formula.
  4. Draft explicit gains-and-losses language and source-treatment language.
  5. Send the draft for administrator pre-review before court entry when possible.
  6. After entry, submit the signed certified order and any required attachments until the alternate payee account is established.

Common Mistakes

  • Using the wrong or shortened plan name instead of the exact legal plan name.
  • Leaving out gains-and-losses language for a participant-directed account.
  • Ignoring separate contribution sources such as Roth, rollover, employer contributions, or stock funds.
  • Failing to address participant loan treatment.
  • Skipping administrator pre-review when public plan procedures are limited or unclear.

FAQ

Does dividing the pSemi Corporation 401(k) Plan in divorce require a QDRO?

Yes. A qualified domestic relations order is required to divide an ERISA-governed 401(k) or profit-sharing plan such as the PSEMI CORPORATION 401(K) PLAN.

Was the recordkeeper identified in public sources?

Not with confidence. Public research did not confirm a current recordkeeper for the PSEMI CORPORATION 401(K) PLAN, so that should be verified directly with the plan administrator.

Why should the order mention gains and losses?

Because this is a defined contribution account whose value can change after the valuation date. The order should say whether the awarded share is adjusted for investment performance until segregation or distribution.

Should the order address separate contribution sources or loans?

Yes. If the participant account includes multiple sources or an outstanding loan, the order should explain how each is treated so the administrator is not left guessing.
