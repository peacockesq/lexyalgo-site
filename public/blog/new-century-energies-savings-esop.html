Corporate History and Plan Status

New Century Energies was a Denver-based energy holding company formed from the merger of Public Service Company of Colorado and Southwestern Public Service Company. In 2000, New Century Energies merged with Excel Corporation (a separate entity) to form Xcel Energy Inc., now one of the largest electric and natural gas utilities in the United States.

As part of the corporate transition, Xcel Energy assumed responsibility for New Century Energies benefit plans. Third-party sources in this research cite a December 31, 2001 effective date for the merger of the New Century Energies savings plan into the broader Xcel Energy 401(k) Savings Plan. However, the New Century Energies plan still appears as a distinct Form 5500 filing under Xcel Energy, which may indicate a legacy plan segment, a separately maintained benefit record, or a plan that was partially merged with some participant cohort remaining.

For active divorce cases involving participants who were covered under New Century Energies, the key drafting question is whether to identify this plan by its original New Century Energies name or by the current Xcel Energy plan name. That question can only be answered by the current plan administrator.

Dividing This Plan in Divorce

This is a defined contribution plan. Participants hold individual accounts that reflect contributions — from the employee and the employer — plus investment experience over time. The account may include an ESOP component holding Xcel Energy company stock, which the full plan name references explicitly.

Identifying the Marital Portion

The marital portion is generally the account balance (or the portion thereof) that accrued during the marriage. State law governs the exact analysis. If the participant has a premarital balance in the account, the parties may need historical account statements to trace the separate property portion. The plan administrator or recordkeeper is typically the starting point for obtaining that history.

ESOP Component Considerations

Plans with ESOP features may hold employer stock in participant accounts. If the participant's account includes Xcel Energy stock, the QDRO should address how company stock is treated — whether the alternate payee receives a proportionate share of stock, an equivalent cash value, or something else. Generic 401(k) language does not always cover this cleanly.

Coverture fractions and 401(k)/ESOP plans: A coverture fraction is a tracing and analysis tool used to calculate the marital portion of an account. It is not a distribution method. The QDRO specifies the award amount or formula. The plan administrator applies the QDRO to calculate the alternate payee's actual share.

QDRO Requirements

A domestic relations order for this plan should at minimum:

  • Identify the plan by its correct current name — confirm whether the plan is now administered under the New Century Energies name or the Xcel Energy 401(k) Savings Plan name before finalizing the order.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with sufficient specificity.
  • Specify the amount, percentage, or formula for the alternate payee's share.
  • State the valuation date or triggering event.
  • Address gains and losses on the alternate payee's share after the valuation date.
  • Address treatment of any company stock (ESOP component) in the account.
  • Address any outstanding loan balance, if applicable.
  • Not require a form of benefit not available under the plan.
Verify plan identity first: Given the corporate merger history and reported plan consolidation, the single most important step before drafting is confirming with Xcel Energy Benefits whether the plan still operates as a standalone plan or whether active participants are now under the Xcel Energy 401(k) Savings Plan. Naming the wrong plan in the QDRO will result in rejection.

Division Formulas

Percentage of vested balance

The alternate payee receives a stated percentage of the participant's vested account balance as of the valuation date, adjusted for gains and losses until segregation. This is the simplest and most common approach for defined contribution plans.

Fixed dollar award

A specific dollar amount can work where the parties have agreed on a number. If the account balance is volatile (e.g., significant company stock position), a fixed dollar award may not account for market movement between the valuation date and plan processing. Gains and losses language is especially important.

Marital share with premarital offset

If the participant had a meaningful pre-marriage account balance, the QDRO can carve out the marital share. This requires the plan or recordkeeper to confirm the premarital balance from their records — request this before drafting.

Stock-specific allocation

If the account holds Xcel Energy company stock through the ESOP component, consider addressing stock specifically: either pro rata allocation of stock shares, or a cash-equivalent assignment of the stock portion's value. Confirm what the plan permits before drafting.

Steps to Divide This Plan

  1. Contact Xcel Energy Benefits to confirm current plan status — whether this plan still operates separately or whether the participant's account is now part of the Xcel Energy 401(k) Savings Plan.
  2. Obtain the most recent account statement and any available QDRO procedures from the plan administrator.
  3. Identify whether the account includes company stock (ESOP component) and how stock shares will be handled.
  4. Determine the division method and valuation date consistent with the divorce settlement or court order.
  5. Draft the QDRO using the correct current plan name and administrator address.
  6. Submit for pre-approval to the plan administrator before court entry, if the plan permits this.
  7. After court entry, submit the certified order and follow up until qualification is confirmed.

Common Mistakes

  • Using the old New Century Energies plan name if the plan has been merged: If the participant's account is now under the Xcel Energy 401(k) Savings Plan, a QDRO naming "New Century Energies" may be rejected or delayed.
  • Ignoring ESOP/company stock: Plans with stock ownership features require attention to how stock is treated. Generic 401(k) language may not address it adequately.
  • Not confirming the plan merge status: The most important pre-drafting step is confirming whether this is still a distinct plan or a merged one. Skipping this creates a correctable but avoidable mistake.
  • Omitting gains and losses language: Account values fluctuate. The order should state clearly how the alternate payee's share is adjusted between valuation date and segregation.

FAQ

Does this plan still exist as a separate plan?

It continues to appear in Form 5500 filings under Xcel Energy Inc. Third-party sources indicate it may have been merged into the Xcel Energy 401(k) Savings Plan in 2001. Confirm current status with Xcel Energy Benefits before drafting any order.

Does it require a QDRO for divorce division?

Yes. It is a qualified ERISA plan. A court order for divorce does not authorize the plan to pay an alternate payee without a qualified QDRO accepted by the administrator.

What does "Savings and Stock Ownership Plan" mean?

The plan combines a 401(k) savings component with an ESOP (Employee Stock Ownership Plan) component. This means the participant's account may include Xcel Energy company stock in addition to other investment holdings.

How do I reach Xcel Energy Benefits?

Xcel Energy's benefits resources are available at xcelenergy.com. Shareholder services can be reached at 1-877-778-6786. For plan-specific QDRO procedures, direct contact with Xcel Energy HR or Benefits is the most reliable route.

Who are "bargaining unit employees" in this context?

Bargaining unit employees are workers covered by a collective bargaining agreement (union employees). New Century Energies operated utility subsidiaries with unionized workforces. The plan's coverage extended to these employees and to some former non-bargaining unit employees, as described in the plan name.
