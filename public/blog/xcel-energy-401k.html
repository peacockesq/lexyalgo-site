401(k) Plans in Divorce

The Xcel Energy 401(k) Savings Plan is a defined contribution plan: the participant has an individual account that grows through contributions and investment returns. In divorce, that means there is an identifiable account balance to divide, which is more straightforward than dividing a pension annuity.

The key drafting questions for any 401(k) QDRO are: (1) how much does the alternate payee receive, (2) as of what valuation date, and (3) what happens to gains and losses between that date and when the plan actually segregates the award. The Xcel Energy plan's Form 5500 benefit codes also indicate an ESOP feature (company stock) and Roth 401(k) contributions — both of which should be addressed if present in the participant's account.

The marital portion of a 401(k) is typically the amount that accrued during the marriage. How that portion is calculated — whether by a formula tied to months of marriage versus total participation, or by a direct dollar or percentage award — depends on the settlement agreement, the court order, and applicable state law. A time-based formula is an analytical tool for identifying the marital share; the QDRO itself specifies the actual award.

QDRO Requirements

A domestic relations order for the Xcel Energy 401(k) Savings Plan must satisfy ERISA's qualification requirements and the plan's own procedures. At minimum, the order should:

  • Identify the correct plan by exact name: Xcel Energy 401(K) Savings Plan.
  • Not confuse this plan with the Xcel Energy pension plan — they are legally separate plans.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with sufficient specificity for administrator review.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula used to calculate the alternate payee's share.
  • Specify the valuation date or event used for calculation.
  • Address whether the assigned share is adjusted for investment gains and losses between the valuation date and segregation.
  • Address treatment of any pre-tax and Roth sub-accounts if both exist in the participant's account.
  • Address any ESOP (company stock) holdings if applicable.
  • Avoid requiring any form or benefit not available under the plan.

Division Formulas

Percentage formula

A percentage award assigns the alternate payee a specified share of the vested account balance as of a stated valuation date, adjusted for gains and losses until segregation. This is common and straightforward for 401(k) accounts where the balance is known.

Fixed dollar formula

A fixed dollar award can work when the settlement uses a specific number. The QDRO should still address gains and losses after the valuation date to avoid disputes during the review period.

Time-based marital share formula

Some orders calculate the marital portion using months of marriage during plan participation relative to total participation months. This is an analytical approach used to identify marital property — it is not a distribution method in itself. The QDRO still specifies the award as a percentage or dollar figure derived from that analysis.

Gains and losses

401(k) accounts move with markets. The order should clearly state whether the alternate payee's share is adjusted for all investment experience from the valuation date through the date of actual segregation or distribution.

Common Mistakes

  • Confusing the 401(k) and pension plans: Xcel Energy has both. A QDRO for the 401(k) does nothing for the pension, and vice versa. If both are at issue, both need separate orders.
  • Ignoring the ESOP component: The Form 5500 indicates an ESOP feature. If the participant holds company stock, the order should address it rather than leaving treatment implied.
  • Leaving gains and losses unstated: In a moving investment account, an award without gains/losses language creates valuation disputes during the review and segregation period.
  • Not addressing Roth sub-accounts: If the participant has Roth contributions, the order should specify whether the alternate payee shares each source proportionally.
  • Relying on unverified contact information: Recordkeeper identity and contact details for this plan are from third-party sources. Confirm with Xcel Energy's HR or benefits department before submitting anything.
