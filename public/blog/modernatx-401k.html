The safest approach: obtain the participant's most recent benefit statement or summary plan description, which will state the exact plan name as maintained by the current plan administrator. Use that name in the QDRO, not a variation from a directory or news source.

401(k) Plans in Divorce

The ModernaTX, Inc. 401(k) Plan is a defined contribution plan. Each participant maintains an individual account that grows through pre-tax deferrals, Roth deferrals, employer matching contributions, and investment returns. In divorce, the account balance can be divided by dollar amount, percentage, or a formula referencing a specified valuation date.

The Form 5500 filing includes benefit code 2S (Roth 401(k)), which means some participants may hold both pre-tax and Roth money in the same plan. The tax treatment of Roth and pre-tax balances differs at distribution, so the QDRO should address each source if both are present — avoiding ambiguity during implementation.

The marital portion of the account is generally the amount that accrued during the marriage. A time-based formula comparing months of marriage during plan participation to total participation months is one way to analyze the marital share — but that is an analytical step, not the distribution method. The QDRO specifies the actual award; the administrator applies the plan's procedures to implement it.

QDRO Requirements

A domestic relations order for the ModernaTX, Inc. 401(k) Plan must satisfy ERISA's requirements and the plan's qualification procedures. At minimum:

  • Identify the plan by its exact legal name (confirm current name with the administrator).
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with sufficient specificity.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula for the alternate payee's share.
  • Specify the valuation date.
  • Address investment gains and losses between the valuation date and segregation.
  • Address Roth and pre-tax sub-accounts if the participant has both.
  • Address any outstanding plan loan balance if applicable.
  • Avoid requiring any benefit not available under the plan.

Division Formulas

Percentage formula

The alternate payee receives a specified percentage of the vested account balance as of the valuation date, adjusted for gains and losses until actual segregation. This is the most common approach for 401(k) accounts with an ascertainable balance.

Fixed dollar formula

A specific dollar amount can be awarded. The order should still say whether gains and losses apply after the valuation date to avoid implementation disputes.

Roth and pre-tax source treatment

If the participant holds both Roth and pre-tax dollars in the plan, the QDRO should state explicitly how each source is allocated. A pro-rata split across all sources in proportion to the overall percentage is a clean approach.

Gains and losses

A 401(k) account can move significantly between the valuation date and the date the plan administrator actually segregates the alternate payee's share. The order should clearly address this to avoid a dispute over whether the alternate payee shares in market movements during the review period.

Common Mistakes

  • Using the wrong plan name: "Moderna Inc." and "ModernaTX, Inc." are not interchangeable in a legal document. Use the exact name confirmed by the current plan administrator.
  • Ignoring Roth sub-accounts: If the participant holds Roth money in the plan, the order must address it. Generic orders often miss this.
  • Leaving gains and losses unstated: Account balances move during the QDRO review period. Silence creates disputes.
  • Using unverified contact information: The John Hancock contact (617-218-1567) is from third-party sources. Confirm directly with Moderna before submitting a QDRO.
  • Skipping administrator pre-review: Without a public model QDRO, a draft review through the administrator before court entry can prevent rejection and amendments.
