Mikuni 401(k) in Divorce

The Mikuni Restaurant Group 401(k) Plan is a defined contribution plan, which means each participant has an individual account rather than a pension formula. The balance changes with investment returns over time. When dividing this type of plan in divorce, the key drafting questions are: what date is used to value the account, what percentage or dollar amount goes to the alternate payee, and what happens to the account between that date and the date the alternate payee's share is actually segregated and distributed.

The plan also includes a profit-sharing component. Employer profit-sharing contributions may vest over time, so the participant's access to those funds may depend on years of service. A QDRO should address whether the alternate payee shares in vested employer contributions only, or whether unvested profit-sharing amounts are also included if and when they vest.

QDRO Requirements

A domestic relations order for the Mikuni Restaurant Group 401(k) Plan must satisfy ERISA and the Internal Revenue Code and pass the plan's own review. A properly drafted order should:

  • Identify the plan by correct name: MIKUNI RESTAURANT GROUP, INC. 401(K) PLAN.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee clearly.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula for the alternate payee's share.
  • Specify the valuation date or triggering event.
  • State whether the alternate payee's share is adjusted for investment gains and losses after the valuation date.
  • Address pre-tax and Roth balances separately if both exist in the account.
  • Address any profit-sharing contributions and vesting, if applicable.
  • Avoid requiring any benefit or payment form not available under the plan.

Division Formulas

Percentage of balance

The alternate payee receives a stated percentage of the participant's vested account balance as of a specified date. Gains and losses on that share can be included through the date of segregation, keeping the award in line with actual account performance.

Fixed dollar amount

The alternate payee receives a specific dollar figure. The order should state whether this amount is adjusted for investment returns between the valuation date and distribution, or whether the award is frozen at the specified dollar amount.

Profit-sharing treatment

If the settlement includes a share of unvested employer contributions, the order should specify that the alternate payee's share of those funds is held until the participant's vesting date or another defined trigger, at which point distribution occurs.

Gains and losses

Explicitly stating whether the alternate payee's share tracks investment returns after the valuation date prevents disputes about value changes during the QDRO review and qualification process.

Steps to Divide the Mikuni 401(k)

  1. Obtain a current account statement showing the total balance, employer contribution sources (profit-sharing), any Roth balances, and any outstanding loan.
  2. Confirm the plan name as MIKUNI RESTAURANT GROUP, INC. 401(K) PLAN and get current QDRO procedures from Mikuni HR.
  3. Choose the division method: percentage of balance, fixed dollar amount, or another clearly stated formula.
  4. Address gains and losses and profit-sharing vesting consistent with the settlement.
  5. Submit a draft order to Mikuni HR for pre-approval before court entry.
  6. After court entry, submit the certified signed order and follow through until the plan confirms qualification and establishes the alternate payee account.

Common Mistakes

  • Ignoring the profit-sharing component: the plan includes both elective deferrals and profit-sharing contributions. Addressing only one type of contribution may leave part of the marital estate unresolved.
  • Leaving gains and losses unstated: a defined contribution account value changes daily. Silence on this point creates disputes about what the alternate payee is actually owed.
  • Using a generic order: plan-specific drafting matters — boilerplate language for a different employer's 401(k) may not work correctly here.
  • Skipping pre-approval: when there is no public model QDRO, draft review with the plan administrator avoids rejections and post-entry court amendments.

FAQ

Does the Mikuni Restaurant Group 401(k) require a QDRO for divorce division?

Yes. A divorce decree alone does not divide an employer-sponsored retirement plan. The Mikuni plan requires a qualified domestic relations order before any benefit can be paid to an alternate payee.

What type of plan is it?

It is a defined contribution profit-sharing plan. Each participant has an individual account, and the balance can grow or shrink based on investment performance and employer profit-sharing contributions.

Does the plan include employer profit-sharing contributions?

The Form 5500 type codes and third-party sources indicate a profit-sharing component. Employer contributions of this type may vest over time. Confirm the current vesting schedule with Mikuni HR before drafting.

Why does gains and losses language matter?

Because account balances change with market performance. The balance at the time the alternate payee's share is segregated may be higher or lower than the value at the valuation date. Clear gains and losses language in the order prevents disputes at that stage.
