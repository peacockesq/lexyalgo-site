In Divorce

The PROLOGIS 401(K) SAVINGS PLAN is a classic defined contribution plan, which makes division more straightforward than a pension in theory and still easy to botch in practice. The usual approach is a percentage award or fixed dollar award as of a stated valuation date, with post-valuation gains and losses applied through segregation.

This plan appears to include more than a plain vanilla pre-tax bucket. The Form 5500 coding and public filings point to Roth capability, participant-directed investments, profit-sharing features, and an employer-stock component. If the order does not say whether the alternate payee receives a pro rata slice of each source and investment bucket, the administrator is left cleaning up ambiguity that should have been fixed in the draft.

QDRO Requirements

A qualifying domestic relations order for the PROLOGIS 401(K) SAVINGS PLAN should be drafted to satisfy ERISA and the plan administrator's current review procedures. At minimum, the order should:

  • Name the plan exactly as shown in the plan documents and Form 5500.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with enough detail for administrator review.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula for the alternate payee award.
  • Specify the valuation date or event used for the calculation.
  • Address investment gains and losses from the valuation date through segregation or distribution.
  • Address separate contribution sources such as pre-tax, Roth, rollover, profit-sharing, or stock components if present.
  • Address participant loan treatment if a loan exists on the account.
  • Avoid awarding any benefit or form of payment the plan does not provide.
  • Public filings support Fidelity administration, but confirm the current Fidelity QDRO workflow and any plan-specific review rules before submission.

Division Formulas

Percentage of account balance

The alternate payee receives a stated percentage of the vested account as of the valuation date, adjusted for gains and losses until segregation. This is usually the cleanest approach for a 401(k)-type plan.

Fixed dollar amount

The alternate payee receives a stated dollar amount. If you use this approach, say explicitly whether that amount is adjusted for gains and losses after the valuation date.

Source-by-source allocation

If the account includes multiple sources such as pre-tax, Roth, rollover, profit-sharing, or employer stock, the order should say whether the award is divided pro rata across each source or by another stated method.

Loan treatment

If the participant has an outstanding plan loan, the order should specify whether the alternate payee share is calculated from the gross balance, the net balance, or another agreed figure.

Steps to Divide

  1. Confirm the exact plan name from the participant's most recent statement or plan materials.
  2. Get the current account statement showing balance, contribution sources, and any outstanding loan.
  3. Confirm whether the participant account includes any employer stock or separate stock fund holdings before finalizing the formula language.
  4. Choose the division formula: percentage award, fixed dollar award, or another clear formula.
  5. Draft explicit gains-and-losses language and source-treatment language.
  6. Send the draft for administrator pre-review before court entry when possible.
  7. After entry, submit the signed certified order and any required attachments until the alternate payee account is established.

Common Mistakes

  • Using the wrong or shortened plan name instead of the exact legal plan name.
  • Leaving out gains-and-losses language for a participant-directed account.
  • Ignoring separate contribution sources such as Roth, rollover, employer contributions, or stock funds.
  • Failing to address participant loan treatment.
  • Skipping administrator pre-review when public plan procedures are limited or unclear.
  • Ignoring a stock fund or employer-stock component that may be held as part of the participant account.

FAQ

Does dividing the Prologis plan require a QDRO?

Yes. A divorce decree by itself does not divide an ERISA 401(k) plan. A qualified domestic relations order is required to assign benefits from the PROLOGIS 401(K) SAVINGS PLAN to an alternate payee.

Who appears to recordkeep the Prologis plan?

Public filings and secondary sources indicate Fidelity entities are involved in plan administration and trust services. Confirm the current recordkeeper and QDRO submission instructions directly before filing.

Should the order mention Roth and employer stock features?

Yes. If those sources exist in the participant account, the order should state how they are divided, usually on a pro rata basis across each source unless the settlement provides something else.

What about gains and losses?

They matter a lot. This is a participant-directed account, so the value can move after the valuation date. The order should say whether the assigned share tracks investment performance until segregation or distribution.

Was an official public model QDRO found?

No. No official public plan-issued model QDRO was located in the research used for this page.
