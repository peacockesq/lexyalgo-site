How 401(k) Plans Are Treated in Divorce

A 401(k) is a defined contribution plan, which means the marital share is generally tied to the account balance and investment performance rather than a promised future pension payment. In a divorce setting, the order usually directs the plan to assign part of the participant's account to the alternate payee.

Once the order is approved, the alternate payee commonly receives a separate account or transfer arrangement inside the plan's system. The exact timing of valuation, earnings treatment, and loan handling should be stated clearly in the order and checked against the administrator's current procedures.

QDRO Requirements

  • Use the precise legal plan name and identify the participant and alternate payee accurately.
  • State the award as a clear percentage or dollar amount and explain whether gains and losses apply.
  • Confirm the valuation date and whether any outstanding participant loan affects the divisible balance.
  • Make sure the order does not require a form of benefit the plan does not offer.
  • Obtain official plan procedures from Fidelity or the plan administrator before submission because no public model or guideline was located.

Division Formulas

For a defined contribution plan like this one, the cleanest drafting usually comes from formulas the administrator can apply directly to an account balance.

Percentage award

Example structure: the alternate payee receives 50% of the vested account balance as of a stated date, adjusted for investment gains and losses from that date to distribution or segregation.

Fixed dollar award

Example structure: the alternate payee receives a stated dollar amount from the account, with express language explaining whether later gains or losses apply before transfer.

The best formula depends on the settlement terms, the available account records, and what the administrator will accept. Precision matters because vague earnings language can create avoidable disputes.

Steps to Divide

  1. Confirm the exact plan identity, participant details, and whether Fidelity is handling the current QDRO intake.
  2. Request the current SPD, any plan-specific QDRO procedures, and any sample or preferred language directly from Fidelity or the plan administrator.
  3. Determine the settlement terms, including valuation date, award format, earnings treatment, and loan treatment.
  4. Draft the QDRO to match both the divorce terms and the plan's actual administrative requirements.
  5. Submit for pre-approval or review if the administrator allows it, then enter the order with the court and provide the signed version for final qualification.

Common Mistakes

Using the wrong plan name

Even small naming errors can delay review or cause the order to be rejected for clarification.

Skipping gains and losses language

If the order does not address post-valuation investment movement, the parties may end up fighting over avoidable math.

Assuming a public model exists

Public research did not surface an official model QDRO or formal guidelines, so the current procedures should be requested directly.

FAQ

Can the Aramark 401(k) Retirement Plan be divided with a QDRO?

Yes. A 401(k) defined contribution plan is typically divided through a QDRO that awards a share of the participant's account to the alternate payee.

Who appears to handle the plan's QDRO process?

Public research indicates Fidelity is the relevant administrator or recordkeeping contact for plan-related QDRO procedures.

Is there a public SPD or official model QDRO available?

No official public SPD PDF, model QDRO, or formal written procedures were identified in the supplied research, so those should be requested directly from the plan.

What does the alternate payee usually receive after approval?

For a defined contribution plan, the alternate payee usually receives a separate account or equivalent transfer arrangement for the awarded share.

What should be verified before drafting?

Verify the current SPD, exact plan name, valuation mechanics, treatment of gains and losses, loan treatment, and Fidelity's current submission rules before finalizing language.
