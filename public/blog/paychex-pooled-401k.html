What a Pooled Employer Plan Means for QDRO Drafting

A Pooled Employer Plan is a specific legal structure created by the SECURE Act of 2019. Multiple unrelated employers — typically small and mid-size businesses — participate in a single plan document through the Pooled Plan Provider (here, Paychex Retirement LLC). The PPP serves as plan administrator and fiduciary for all participating employers' sub-accounts under one umbrella plan.

This has practical implications for divorce drafting that differ from a typical single-employer 401(k):

  • The participant's employer is not the plan. The participant works for a business that adopted the PEP. Paychex Retirement LLC — not the participant's employer — is the administrator of record.
  • QDRO review applies the uniform PEP plan document. The review is conducted under the PEP's terms, not a plan document specific to the participant's employer.
  • Plan identification in the QDRO matters. The order should identify the plan as the Paychex Pooled Employer 401(k) Plan and the plan sponsor/administrator as Paychex Retirement LLC — not the participant's day-to-day employer.
  • Participating employer sub-account identification: Whether the QDRO must separately identify the participating employer's sub-account, or whether PEP-level procedures apply uniformly, was not confirmed in this research. Confirm this with Paychex Retirement Services before drafting.
Do not confuse this plan with Paychex's own internal plan. Paychex also operates a separate 401(k) plan for its own employees (Paychex Inc. 401(k) Incentive Retirement Plan). The Paychex Pooled Employer 401(k) Plan (EIN 85-3275179) is distinct — it covers participants at other employers, not Paychex employees. Verify the correct plan before drafting.

QDRO Requirements

Based on the official Paychex Pooled Employer Plan Service Agreement and applicable ERISA requirements, a domestic relations order for this plan must:

  • Identify the plan by name: Paychex Pooled Employer 401(k) Plan.
  • Identify the plan administrator: Paychex Retirement LLC.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with sufficient detail for administrator processing.
  • State the award as a specific dollar amount or percentage of the participant's account balance as of a stated date. Coverture formula is not accepted as the distribution method.
  • Specify the valuation date or triggering event.
  • Address whether the assigned share is adjusted for investment gains and losses between the valuation date and the date of segregation.
  • Address pre-tax and Roth source allocation if the participant holds both types of contributions.
  • Avoid requiring any form or benefit not available under the plan.

The QDRO processing fee is charged to the participant's account upon qualification unless the order specifies a different allocation. If the parties want to allocate the fee differently, the order should say so explicitly.

Division Formulas

Percentage of account balance

The standard approach for this type of defined contribution plan. The order states the alternate payee receives a set percentage of the participant's vested account balance as of the valuation date, adjusted for investment gains and losses through segregation. Simple, clean, administrator-friendly.

Fixed dollar amount

A specific dollar amount can be used where the parties agree on a hard number. The order should address whether that amount accrues investment earnings from the valuation date through segregation. A frozen fixed amount that does not track investment performance can create unexpected outcomes if segregation takes months.

Gains and losses clause

For a defined contribution account, this clause prevents the awarded share from becoming disconnected from actual account performance between valuation and segregation. Without it, the result depends on the administrator's interpretation of silence — which is not a good position to be in.

Pre-tax and Roth allocation

If the participant's account includes both pre-tax and Roth contributions, the order should specify how the award is allocated across those sources. A pro rata split is the most common and cleanest approach. The Paychex PEP plan features include Roth options, so this may be relevant.

Steps to Divide the Paychex PEP Account

  1. Confirm the participant's retirement account is in the Paychex Pooled Employer 401(k) Plan (EIN 85-3275179) and not the separate Paychex Inc. employee plan.
  2. Obtain the participant's current account statement, including total balance, pre-tax and Roth sub-balances, and vesting status.
  3. Contact Paychex Retirement Services directly to confirm the current QDRO procedures, submission address, processing fee amounts, and any model order language.
  4. Confirm whether the QDRO must identify the participating employer's sub-account within the PEP or whether the plan-level designation is sufficient.
  5. Draft the order with a clear award formula, valuation date, gains/losses clause, source allocation, and QDRO fee allocation.
  6. Submit for pre-approval review if Paychex allows it, before court entry.
  7. After court entry, submit the certified order with required supporting documents and follow up until qualification is confirmed and the alternate payee's account is established.

Common Mistakes

  • Naming the participant's employer instead of Paychex Retirement LLC as the administrator: The participant's day-to-day employer is not the plan administrator. The PEP administrator is Paychex Retirement LLC.
  • Confusing this plan with Paychex's own employee plan: The Paychex Inc. internal 401(k) and the Paychex Pooled Employer 401(k) Plan are separate plans with different EINs. Make sure the correct plan is identified.
  • Using a coverture formula: Not accepted. Award as a dollar amount or percentage as of a stated date.
  • Leaving the QDRO fee unaddressed: The plan charges a QDRO processing fee to the participant's account unless the order says otherwise. If the parties want a different allocation, say so in the order.
  • Skipping confirmation on sub-account identification: For a PEP with hundreds of participating employers, it may matter whether the QDRO must reference the participant's specific employer sub-account. Confirm with Paychex before filing.

FAQ

What is the Paychex Pooled Employer 401(k) Plan?

A Pooled Employer Plan (PEP) established under the SECURE Act, where multiple unrelated employers participate in a single plan document. Paychex Retirement LLC is the Pooled Plan Provider and plan administrator. The participant is not employed by Paychex — they work for a business that adopted the PEP.

Does this plan require a QDRO?

Yes. An ERISA 401(k) plan cannot be divided by a divorce decree alone. The Paychex PEP requires a qualifying domestic relations order before any benefit can be assigned to an alternate payee.

How should the award be structured?

As a specific dollar amount or percentage of the participant's account balance as of a stated date. The marital coverture formula is not accepted as a distribution method for this defined contribution plan.

Are there QDRO processing fees?

Yes, per the Paychex PEP Service Agreement. Paychex (or its designee) charges a QDRO processing fee, deducted from the participant's plan account after qualification unless the order specifies otherwise. Confirm current amounts with Paychex Retirement Services.

Who do I contact for QDRO procedures?

Contact Paychex Retirement Services directly — not general Paychex payroll or HR support. No official public model QDRO was found in this research, so direct contact is required for current procedures and submission information.
