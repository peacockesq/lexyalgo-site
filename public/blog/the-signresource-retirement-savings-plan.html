401(k) in Divorce

The SignResource Retirement Savings Plan is a defined contribution plan, meaning each participant holds an individual account balance. In divorce, the alternate payee is typically awarded either a percentage or a fixed dollar amount of that balance as of a specified valuation date. The order needs to be clear enough that the administrator can implement it without having to contact anyone for clarification.

Because the recordkeeper was not confirmed in public research, pre-review by the plan administrator before court entry is especially important here. That step catches drafting problems before they become rejection letters.

QDRO Requirements

A qualifying domestic relations order for The SignResource Retirement Savings Plan must meet ERISA's minimum content requirements and whatever specific procedures the plan administrator applies. At minimum, the order should:

  • Name the plan exactly: THE SIGNRESOURCE RETIREMENT SAVINGS PLAN.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee clearly enough for administrator review.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula used to determine the alternate payee's share.
  • Specify the valuation date or event controlling the calculation.
  • Address gains and losses from the valuation date through segregation or distribution.
  • Address source treatment if multiple contribution types are present in the account.
  • Address participant loan treatment if a loan exists.
  • Avoid requiring any benefit form or option the plan does not offer.

Division Formulas

Percentage award

The alternate payee receives a stated percentage of the participant's vested account balance as of the valuation date, adjusted for gains and losses through segregation. This is the standard approach for defined contribution plans.

Fixed dollar amount

The alternate payee receives a specific dollar figure. The order should address whether that amount is adjusted for gains and losses from the valuation date; silence on this point invites disputes later.

Multiple source allocation

If the account includes employer contributions, profit sharing, and employee deferrals as separate sub-accounts, specifying which sources are divided and in what proportion is cleaner than a single global percentage with ambiguous scope.

Loan balance treatment

If there is an outstanding participant loan, state whether the award is calculated from the gross balance before the loan or the net balance after. That decision affects what the alternate payee actually receives.

Common Mistakes

  • Wrong plan name: using shorthand instead of the exact Form 5500 plan name.
  • No gains and losses language: the account moves after the valuation date; the order should address what happens to the alternate payee's share.
  • Not confirming the administrator: since the recordkeeper is unconfirmed in public sources, direct contact before drafting is essential.
  • Overlooking the loan: an outstanding participant loan changes the economics of the award.
  • Skipping pre-review: when public procedures are unavailable, pre-review can prevent rejection and a second court appearance.

FAQ

Does dividing this plan in divorce require a QDRO?

Yes. A divorce decree alone cannot divide an ERISA defined contribution plan. A qualified domestic relations order meeting federal requirements and the plan's procedures is required.

Who processes QDROs for this plan?

The recordkeeper was not confirmed in public research. Contact SignResource, LLC directly for current administrator and QDRO submission information.

Should the order address multiple contribution sources?

Yes if the account has separate employer and employee sub-accounts. Specifying how each source is allocated is cleaner than a global percentage that leaves source treatment ambiguous.

Why does gains and losses language matter?

Because participant-directed accounts move with investment performance. The order should specify whether the alternate payee's share adjusts for gains and losses between the valuation date and segregation.
