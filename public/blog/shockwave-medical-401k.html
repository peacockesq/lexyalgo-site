401(k) in Divorce

The Shockwave Medical 401(k) plan is a defined contribution plan. In divorce, the portion of the account that accrued during the marriage may be divisible through a Qualified Domestic Relations Order. The account balance — not a future pension promise — is the asset being divided. Because the balance changes with investment performance, gains and losses between the valuation date and the date the alternate payee's share is actually segregated should be addressed in the order.

The most significant threshold issue for a Shockwave Medical 401(k) in a divorce proceeding is plan identity. The Johnson & Johnson acquisition closed in mid-2024. If the participant was employed at Shockwave Medical before the acquisition and has since transitioned to J&J, the plan may have been merged or renamed. Submitting a QDRO to an outdated plan administrator can result in rejection and delay. Confirm the current plan name and administrator before drafting.

QDRO Requirements

A domestic relations order for a Shockwave Medical 401(k) account must satisfy ERISA, the Internal Revenue Code, and the plan's qualification procedures — including any changes made after the J&J acquisition. At minimum, the order should:

  • Confirm and identify the correct current plan name — the plan may no longer be called the Shockwave Medical, Inc. 401(k) Plan if it has been merged into J&J's retirement structure.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with sufficient specificity for administrator review.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula used to determine the alternate payee's share.
  • Specify the valuation date or triggering event.
  • Address whether the assigned share is adjusted for gains and losses after the valuation date.
  • Address pre-tax and Roth balances separately if both are present.
  • Address any outstanding loan balance.
  • Not require any benefit not otherwise available under the plan.
Practice point: Given the 2024 acquisition, draft review through the current plan administrator is especially important here. Do not submit a QDRO naming the pre-acquisition entity if the plan has been absorbed into J&J's benefit structure. Contact J&J's benefits administration or Shockwave Medical's former HR channel to identify the correct QDRO submission path.

Division Formulas

Percentage formula

Assigning the alternate payee a percentage of the participant's vested account balance as of a specified date is the most common approach for defined contribution plans. The order should state whether that percentage is adjusted for investment performance between the valuation date and segregation.

Fixed dollar formula

A fixed dollar amount is straightforward but requires clarity on whether gains and losses apply after the valuation date. Leaving gains and losses unstated is a common source of post-entry disputes in defined contribution plan QDROs.

Gains and losses treatment

From the valuation date to the date of actual account segregation, the balance can move significantly. The order should explicitly state whether the alternate payee's share is adjusted for investment gains and losses during the review and segregation period.

Pre-tax vs. Roth

If the participant's account holds both pre-tax and Roth balances, the order should address each source type. A pro rata division — where the alternate payee receives the stated percentage of each source — is often the cleanest method.

Steps to Divide the Plan

  1. Confirm plan identity first. Contact J&J's benefits administration or the former Shockwave Medical HR team to confirm the current plan name, administrator, and QDRO review channel. Do not assume the pre-acquisition plan name and administrator are still correct.
  2. Gather the participant's most recent account statement, including pre-tax balance, Roth balance, employer contributions, and any loan balance.
  3. Determine the division formula and valuation date based on the divorce settlement.
  4. Draft the QDRO using the current plan's specific requirements and submit for administrator pre-review before court entry.
  5. Obtain court entry of the signed domestic relations order.
  6. Submit the certified, court-entered order to the plan administrator and follow through to account segregation.

Common Mistakes

  • Submitting to the wrong plan: if the plan was merged into J&J following the 2024 acquisition, a QDRO naming the pre-acquisition plan may be rejected.
  • Skipping administrator confirmation: no public model QDRO exists for this plan, and plan terms may have changed post-acquisition. Administrator contact is not optional — it is the only reliable way to get current procedures.
  • Leaving gains and losses unaddressed: standard for defined contribution plans; an order that is silent on this point creates avoidable disputes.
  • Ignoring Roth balances: if both pre-tax and Roth money are in the account, the order should specify how each is divided.
  • Not addressing outstanding loans: a loan balance reduces the account's effective value; the order should be clear whether the alternate payee's share is calculated on the gross or net balance.

FAQ

Does the Shockwave Medical 401(k) require a QDRO to divide in divorce?

Yes. As an ERISA-qualified defined contribution plan, a qualifying domestic relations order reviewed and approved by the plan administrator is required before any distribution to an alternate payee. A divorce judgment alone does not transfer retirement plan benefits.

What happened to the Shockwave Medical plan after the J&J acquisition?

Johnson & Johnson completed its acquisition of Shockwave Medical on May 31, 2024. The plan may have been merged into J&J's retirement benefit structure. Current plan name, administrator, and QDRO procedures need to be confirmed directly before any drafting work begins.

Who processed QDROs for this plan before the acquisition?

Third-party sources identify Fidelity Investments as the pre-acquisition recordkeeper. No official SPD or model QDRO was found. If Fidelity remains the recordkeeper under whatever the current plan structure is, the Fidelity QDRO Center at qdro.fidelity.com would be the starting point for QDRO procedures.

Can the Shockwave Medical 401(k) balance be divided in full?

The entire vested account balance — including employee contributions, employer matching, and any investment gains — can potentially be awarded in a QDRO, subject to the terms of the divorce settlement and plan rules. Unvested employer contributions may or may not be transferable depending on the plan's vesting provisions.

Where do I start if I'm dealing with a Shockwave Medical 401(k) in a divorce?

Start by contacting J&J's benefits administration or Shockwave Medical's former HR to get current plan information. Identify the current plan name, administrator, and QDRO review channel before doing any drafting. This is not a case where a generic 401(k) template will reliably work without verification.
