401(k) in Divorce

The Envirovac Holdings 401(k) Savings Plan is a defined contribution plan — the participant holds an individual account that can be divided by percentage or dollar amount as of a specific date. Like the related Profit Sharing Plan, it includes both a 401(k) savings component and a profit sharing element, and Roth contributions are available.

The most important first step in any Envirovac QDRO is confirming which plan the participant is enrolled in. If the participant has only one account, the question is straightforward. Some employees may participate in both plans. A QDRO for the Savings Plan must name it exactly — using the Profit Sharing Plan's name by mistake, or using a generic "Envirovac 401(k)" description, can cause rejection and delays.

Sponsor change caution: One third-party source indicates the plan sponsor may have changed to Enviro Hydro Holdings, Inc. as of May 22, 2024. If correct, the plan name in a QDRO may need to reflect the new sponsor entity. Verify the current plan name with Lincoln Financial or the plan administrator before drafting.

QDRO Requirements

A qualifying domestic relations order for the Envirovac Holdings 401(k) Savings Plan must satisfy ERISA's minimum content requirements and Lincoln Financial's procedures. The order should:

  • Name the correct plan exactly — confirm the current name first, given the potential sponsor change.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with sufficient specificity for administrator review.
  • State the dollar amount, percentage, or formula for the alternate payee's share.
  • Specify the valuation date or triggering event for the calculation.
  • Address investment gains and losses from the valuation date through segregation or distribution.
  • Address vesting — whether the award covers the vested balance only or includes unvested amounts contingent on future vesting.
  • Address pre-tax and Roth source treatment if both exist in the account.
  • Address outstanding loan balance treatment if applicable.
Lincoln Financial QDRO process: Upon receiving written notice of a pending order, Lincoln Financial may place a Domestic Relations Restriction (DRR) on the participant's account. Once the order is reviewed and qualified by the LNC Benefits Committee, a separate account is established for the alternate payee. Contact Lincoln's QDRO Compliance Team for current procedures and sample language.

Division Formulas

Percentage of vested account

The alternate payee receives a stated percentage of the participant's total vested account balance as of the valuation date, adjusted for gains and losses until segregation. This is the most common approach for defined contribution plans and covers all contribution sources proportionally.

Fixed dollar amount

A specific dollar amount can work when the settlement uses a hard number. Address whether gains and losses apply between the valuation date and segregation — silence on this point leaves the award value uncertain over time.

Vesting treatment

If the profit sharing component of this plan is not fully vested, address how unvested balances are treated. Limiting the award to the currently vested balance and stating that explicitly is typically the cleanest approach.

Loan balance treatment

If the participant has an outstanding 401(k) loan, specify whether the alternate payee's share is calculated from the gross account balance before subtracting the loan, or from the net balance after. State it clearly rather than leaving it for the administrator to infer.

Steps to Divide

  1. Confirm which Envirovac plan the participant is enrolled in — Savings Plan or Profit Sharing Plan (or both) — and confirm the current plan name given the potential 2024 sponsor change.
  2. Obtain the participant's current benefit statement for the Savings Plan showing vested/unvested balances, pre-tax and Roth sources, and any outstanding loan.
  3. Contact Lincoln Financial's QDRO Compliance Team for current procedures, the correct plan name to use, and any available sample language.
  4. Choose the division formula: percentage of vested account, fixed dollar, or another clearly stated method.
  5. Draft gains/losses, vesting, and source treatment language consistent with the settlement.
  6. Submit a pre-review draft to Lincoln Financial before court entry when possible.
  7. After court entry, submit the certified order and follow up until the alternate payee's account is confirmed.

Common Mistakes

  • Wrong plan — Savings vs. Profit Sharing: Envirovac has two plans. Using the Profit Sharing Plan's name when you mean the Savings Plan (or vice versa) will cause rejection. Confirm the specific plan first.
  • Wrong sponsor name: if the plan sponsor changed to Enviro Hydro Holdings, Inc. in 2024, using the old Envirovac Holdings, LLC name without verifying may create a plan identification problem.
  • No gains and losses language: participant-directed accounts change daily. Without explicit language, the award value is uncertain between the valuation date and the date of segregation.
  • Ignoring vesting: if the profit sharing component is not fully vested, failing to address unvested balances leaves the alternate payee's interest on those amounts unresolved.
  • Skipping pre-review: Lincoln Financial has its own sample language and review process. Working with their materials before court entry avoids rejection and amendments afterward.

FAQ

Does dividing this plan require a QDRO?

Yes. The Envirovac Holdings 401(k) Savings Plan is ERISA-governed. A divorce decree alone cannot direct it to pay benefits to an alternate payee. A qualifying domestic relations order is required.

What is the difference between the Savings Plan and the Profit Sharing Plan?

They are two separate plans under the same employer EIN. The Savings Plan (about 319 participants) and the Profit Sharing Plan (about 435 participants) are distinct plans. A QDRO must name the correct one. Confirm which plan the participant holds before drafting.

Who handles QDRO review?

What happens after Lincoln receives the order?
