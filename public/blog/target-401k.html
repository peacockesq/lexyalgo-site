Employer Stock / ESOP Feature: A Drafting Flag

The Target Corporation 401(k) Plan's Form 5500 type codes include indicators associated with employer stock and ESOP-related provisions. This is an important flag for QDRO drafting because it introduces a potential additional layer of complexity that does not exist in a plain defined contribution plan.

If Target stock is currently an active investment option in the participant's account:

  • The account may hold Target shares as a distinct investment, not just mutual fund units. A QDRO dividing the account may need to address whether employer securities are divided in-kind or liquidated for purposes of transfer to the alternate payee.
  • Some plans restrict the alternate payee's ability to hold employer stock; others allow it. The plan's own rules — confirmed from Alight Solutions or the Target Benefits Center — control.
  • The order should avoid language that would require treatment of employer securities in a way the plan does not permit.

Whether the employer stock/ESOP feature is currently active or was discontinued is not confirmed in the research used for this page. This is a confirmed gap that requires direct verification before drafting.

Confirm with the administrator before drafting: Contact the Target Benefits Center (800-828-5850) or Alight Solutions to confirm whether employer stock is currently a plan investment option and what the plan rules say about dividing employer securities in a QDRO. Do not assume this feature is inactive just because it is not prominently described in third-party plan summaries.

Dividing the Target 401(k) in Divorce

The Target Corporation 401(k) Plan is a defined contribution plan — the participant holds an individual account that reflects their own contributions, any employer contributions, and investment performance over time. In divorce, the account is divided by specifying either a percentage of the vested balance or a fixed dollar amount, measured at a stated valuation date.

Because the account value changes with investment performance, the order should address whether the alternate payee's share is adjusted for gains and losses between the valuation date and the date of actual segregation. The order should also address pre-tax and Roth source allocation, as the plan includes both contribution types.

No coverture formula: The Target 401(k) is a defined contribution plan. The marital coverture fraction is an analytical tool for separate property tracing — it is not the distribution method in the QDRO. The order must express the award as a specific dollar amount or percentage of the account balance as of a stated date.

QDRO Requirements

A domestic relations order for the Target Corporation 401(k) Plan must satisfy ERISA and Alight Solutions' plan-specific review requirements. At minimum, the order should:

  • Identify the plan by name: Target Corporation 401(k) Plan.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with sufficient specificity for Alight to locate the account and process the order.
  • State the award as a specific dollar amount or percentage of the vested account balance as of a stated valuation date.
  • Address investment gains and losses from the valuation date through actual segregation.
  • Address pre-tax and Roth source allocation.
  • Address employer stock / ESOP provisions if Target shares are in the participant's account — confirm the current plan rules on employer securities in QDROs with the administrator first.
  • Avoid requiring any benefit not available under the plan.
  • Comply with any current Alight Solutions format or submission requirements for QDRO review.

Division Formulas

Percentage of vested balance

Assign the alternate payee a set percentage of the participant's vested account balance as of the valuation date. A gains-and-losses clause extends that percentage through actual segregation. This is the standard approach for defined contribution plans and the most administrator-friendly formula.

Fixed dollar amount

A flat dollar amount works when the settlement uses a hard number. The order should specify whether the amount is static or adjusted for plan investment returns from the valuation date through segregation. A static amount can produce unintended results if account performance changes meaningfully before the order is processed.

Employer stock handling

If Target stock is in the participant's account, the order should address how that portion is divided — whether shares are transferred in kind to the alternate payee's account, or whether that portion is liquidated before transfer. Confirm the plan's rules on employer securities from Alight or the Target Benefits Center before specifying a method.

Pre-tax and Roth allocation

Where the participant's account includes both pre-tax and Roth contributions, the order should state how the award is allocated across those sources. Pro rata allocation — dividing each source at the same percentage as the overall award — is the most common and clearest approach.

Common Mistakes

  • Ignoring the employer stock flag: The Form 5500 type codes flag employer stock and ESOP-related features. If Target stock is in the account, failing to address it in the QDRO can create complications during segregation.
  • Using a coverture formula: Not accepted. The award must be a dollar amount or percentage as of a stated valuation date.
  • Omitting gains and losses language: A defined contribution account changes value daily with the market. Without a gains-and-losses clause, the alternate payee's share may not reflect actual economic performance between the valuation date and segregation.
  • Not confirming the Alight QDRO submission channel: Alight Solutions' specific QDRO submission process (portal, mailing address, or other channel) was not confirmed in this research. Sending to the wrong channel can delay qualification. Confirm before filing.
  • Skipping pre-approval review: Where available, pre-approval review before court entry catches problems before the order is signed — saving the parties the time and expense of a court amendment.
