Taft-Hartley Plan: What It Means for Divorce Drafting

This is a jointly trusteed Taft-Hartley plan, which means it is governed by a Board of Trustees composed of equal numbers of Teamsters union representatives and UPS management representatives. The plan is not a standard corporate 401(k) — it is a collectively bargained benefit plan with its own governance structure and trust documents.

For QDRO purposes, the jointly trusteed structure does not change the basic mechanics: it is still a defined contribution plan with individual participant accounts. The award still needs to be stated as a dollar amount or percentage of the account balance as of a valuation date. What the structure does affect is administrator identity — the Board of Trustees and its designee (Empower) are the administrators, not a corporate HR department. The submission goes through upsqdro.com, not a standard HR QDRO inbox.

The upsqdro.com Official Portal

UPS operates a dedicated QDRO resource website at www.upsqdro.com that covers multiple UPS plans, including the Teamster-UPS National 401(k) Tax Deferred Savings Plan. This is the official channel for QDRO procedures, model orders, and submission. Key features of the portal:

  • Model QDRO download: Plan-specific model QDROs are available from the site. To download, the requester must provide participant or submitter identification information (name, email, SSN, birthdate, or submitter credentials).
  • Administrative hold on download: When a model QDRO or procedures packet is downloaded, an administrative hold is placed on the participant's account. This restricts certain account transactions while the QDRO process is pending. Parties and counsel should understand that requesting the model order triggers this hold.
  • Pre-approval strongly encouraged: UPS strongly encourages submitting a draft order for review before court entry. This allows the administrator to flag qualification issues before the order is signed and entered, reducing the need for post-entry amendments.
  • Electronic submission preferred: QDRO submission through upsqdro.com is the preferred and official route. Mail submissions are also accepted (55 Glenlake Parkway, Atlanta, GA 30328) but electronic is faster.
Source quality assessment for this plan: STRONG. The upsqdro.com site is a genuine official resource. The plan is above average in QDRO procedure transparency — model orders are available, the submission channel is clear, and the pre-approval option is explicitly encouraged. QDRO Requirements

A domestic relations order for the Teamster-UPS National 401(k) Plan must satisfy ERISA and the plan's qualification procedures. Based on the available research, the order should:

  • Identify the plan by exact name: The Teamster-UPS National 401(k) Tax Deferred Savings Plan.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with sufficient information for Empower and the Board of Trustees to process the order.
  • State the award as a specific dollar amount or percentage of the participant's vested account balance as of a stated valuation date. Coverture formula is not the distribution method.
  • Address whether the alternate payee's share is adjusted for investment gains and losses between the valuation date and the date of segregation.
  • Address pre-tax and Roth source allocation if applicable.
  • Follow the format of the model QDRO available from upsqdro.com to maximize compatibility with plan review procedures.

Because UPS provides a model QDRO, using that model as a foundation is strongly recommended over drafting from scratch. Deviations from the model format should be deliberate and clearly supported by the facts of the case.

Division Formulas

Percentage of vested balance

Award the alternate payee a stated percentage of the participant's vested account balance as of the valuation date. Gains and losses should be addressed: if the share tracks investment performance, the alternate payee's economic result is not frozen by market timing. Most 401(k) model orders include a gains-and-losses clause.

Fixed dollar amount

A flat dollar amount is clear, but the order should specify whether that amount is adjusted for earnings from the valuation date through segregation. Without an earnings clause on a fixed dollar award, the outcome depends on how quickly the plan processes the order.

Use the UPS model QDRO

The model QDRO available from upsqdro.com is the plan's preferred starting point. Using the model reduces rejection risk and speeds review. Adapt it to the facts of the case — do not use a generic 401(k) order and assume it will qualify.

Pre-tax and Roth allocation

The Teamster-UPS 401(k) plan type includes both pre-tax and Roth contribution features. If the participant has both sources, the order should address how the award is split across them. A pro rata split across all sources is the most straightforward approach.

Steps to Divide the Teamster-UPS 401(k)

  1. Confirm the participant is in the Teamster-UPS National 401(k) Tax Deferred Savings Plan (EIN 95-1732075) — not the separate non-union UPS 401(k) Savings Plan. Check the participant's account statement or Empower records.
  2. Visit www.upsqdro.com and download the model QDRO and procedures for this plan. Note that downloading places an administrative hold on the participant's account — advise the client accordingly before requesting the model.
  3. Review the plan's official QDRO procedures and use the model order as the drafting foundation.
  4. Adapt the model to the facts of the case: division percentage or dollar amount, valuation date, gains/losses treatment, pre-tax and Roth allocation.
  5. Submit the draft order through upsqdro.com for pre-approval review before court presentation. UPS strongly encourages this step.
  6. After address any pre-approval comments and enter the order in court. Submit the certified order through upsqdro.com or by mail to 55 Glenlake Parkway, Atlanta, Georgia 30328.
  7. Follow up until the plan issues a written qualification determination and the alternate payee's account is established with Empower.

Common Mistakes

  • Using the wrong UPS plan: There are multiple UPS 401(k) plans. The Teamster-UPS plan covers union members. Non-union UPS employees have a different plan. Confirm the correct plan from the participant's account records before drafting.
  • Not using the upsqdro.com model: A generic 401(k) order may not satisfy this plan's specific requirements. The model is available and the plan prefers it. Use it.
  • Not advising the client about the administrative hold: The hold is triggered by downloading the model QDRO from upsqdro.com. Clients should know this before the request is made so they can plan accordingly.
  • Skipping pre-approval: UPS strongly encourages pre-approval review. Skipping it increases the risk of post-entry rejection, which requires court re-entry and more delay.
  • Coverture formula as the distribution method: This is a defined contribution plan. The award must be a dollar amount or percentage as of a valuation date, not a marital fraction applied against accrued benefits.

FAQ

Who is covered by the Teamster-UPS National 401(k) Plan?

Eligible part-time and full-time UPS employees who are members of the Teamsters union. Non-union UPS employees have a separate plan. Confirm from the participant's own statements before drafting.

Where do I get the official model QDRO?

From www.upsqdro.com. Download requires participant or submitter authentication. Downloading triggers an administrative hold on the participant's account.

What does the administrative hold do?

It restricts account transactions while the QDRO process is pending. The hold begins when the model QDRO or procedures packet is downloaded from upsqdro.com. Advise clients before triggering it.

Is pre-approval required?

Not strictly required, but UPS strongly encourages submitting a draft for review before court entry. Given the risk of post-entry rejection, pre-approval is worth doing.

Can I use a coverture formula for this plan?

No. The Teamster-UPS 401(k) is a defined contribution plan. The award must be a specific dollar amount or percentage of the vested balance as of a stated valuation date. Coverture fractions are not the distribution method.
