In Divorce

The MODERNATX, INC. 401(K) PLAN is a defined contribution account, so the participant has an individual balance instead of a pension formula. In divorce, that usually means the account can be divided by percentage or by fixed dollar amount as of a stated valuation date, but the order still has to do the boring precision work: exact plan identification, gains and losses language, and source-by-source treatment if the account includes both traditional and Roth money.

This plan is coded as a 401(k) arrangement with profit-sharing, participant direction, and automatic enrollment. That matters because market movement after the valuation date can materially change the account before segregation. If the participant also has a plan loan, silence on loan treatment is how you end up with a fight nobody wanted.

QDRO Requirements

A qualifying domestic relations order for the MODERNATX, INC. 401(K) PLAN should be drafted to satisfy ERISA and the plan administrator's current review procedures. At minimum, the order should:

  • Name the plan exactly as shown in the plan documents and Form 5500.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with enough detail for administrator review.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula for the alternate payee award.
  • Specify the valuation date or event used for the calculation.
  • Address investment gains and losses from the valuation date through segregation or distribution.
  • Address separate contribution sources such as pre-tax, Roth, rollover, profit-sharing, or stock components if present.
  • Address participant loan treatment if a loan exists on the account.
  • Avoid awarding any benefit or form of payment the plan does not provide.
  • Secondary sources indicate John Hancock may be the recordkeeper. Confirm the current recordkeeper, QDRO review address, and whether pre-approval review is available before submitting anything.

Division Formulas

Percentage of account balance

The alternate payee receives a stated percentage of the vested account as of the valuation date, adjusted for gains and losses until segregation. This is usually the cleanest approach for a 401(k)-type plan.

Fixed dollar amount

The alternate payee receives a stated dollar amount. If you use this approach, say explicitly whether that amount is adjusted for gains and losses after the valuation date.

Source-by-source allocation

If the account includes multiple sources such as pre-tax, Roth, rollover, profit-sharing, or employer stock, the order should say whether the award is divided pro rata across each source or by another stated method.

Loan treatment

If the participant has an outstanding plan loan, the order should specify whether the alternate payee share is calculated from the gross balance, the net balance, or another agreed figure.

Steps to Divide

  1. Confirm the exact plan name from the participant's most recent statement or plan materials.
  2. Get the current account statement showing balance, contribution sources, and any outstanding loan.
  3. Ask the plan administrator to confirm the current recordkeeper and QDRO review procedure before circulating the draft for signature.
  4. Choose the division formula: percentage award, fixed dollar award, or another clear formula.
  5. Draft explicit gains-and-losses language and source-treatment language.
  6. Send the draft for administrator pre-review before court entry when possible.
  7. After entry, submit the signed certified order and any required attachments until the alternate payee account is established.

Common Mistakes

  • Using the wrong or shortened plan name instead of the exact legal plan name.
  • Leaving out gains-and-losses language for a participant-directed account.
  • Ignoring separate contribution sources such as Roth, rollover, employer contributions, or stock funds.
  • Failing to address participant loan treatment.
  • Skipping administrator pre-review when public plan procedures are limited or unclear.
  • Assuming a directory-listed recordkeeper is current without checking the latest plan materials.

FAQ

Does dividing the MODERNATX plan in divorce require a QDRO?

Yes. A divorce judgment alone does not divide an ERISA-governed 401(k) plan. A qualified domestic relations order is required before an alternate payee can receive a transfer or distribution from the MODERNATX, INC. 401(K) PLAN.

Who handles QDRO review for the MODERNATX plan?

Secondary sources point to John Hancock, but this research did not locate an official public procedure sheet or SPD confirming that. Verify the current recordkeeper and submission process directly with the plan administrator before drafting.

Should the order address Roth and pre-tax balances separately?

Yes, if both sources exist. The cleanest approach is usually a pro rata award across each source unless the settlement says otherwise.

Why do gains and losses matter for this plan?

Because this is a participant-directed account. The balance can move between the valuation date and the date the alternate payee share is actually segregated, so the order should say whether the assigned share tracks investment performance.

Was an official public model QDRO found?

No. No official public model QDRO was identified in the research used for this page.
