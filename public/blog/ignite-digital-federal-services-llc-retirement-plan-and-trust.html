In Divorce

This is still a defined contribution account even though the feature list is longer than usual. The participant has an account balance, not a monthly pension promise, so division is typically accomplished by awarding a percentage or fixed dollar amount as of a stated date. That part is straightforward. The drafting details are where people make a mess.

Because the plan reportedly includes multiple contribution features, the order should be written broadly enough to reach all divisible account sources that the parties intend to split. If the participant has Roth money, employer contributions, rollover funds, or a loan, the order should say how those are handled. Otherwise the administrator is stuck interpreting a settlement that should have been drafted cleanly the first time.

QDRO Requirements

A qualifying domestic relations order for this plan should do the following:

  • Name the plan exactly: IGNITE DIGITAL FEDERAL SERVICES LLC RETIREMENT PLAN AND TRUST.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee clearly enough for administrator review.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula used to determine the alternate payee's share.
  • Specify the valuation date or event used for the calculation.
  • Address gains and losses from the valuation date through segregation or distribution.
  • Address all intended account sources clearly, including Roth or employer contribution sources if applicable.
  • Address any outstanding participant loan if one exists.
  • Avoid requiring any form of benefit or distribution the plan does not permit.

Division Formulas

Percentage award

A percentage of the vested account balance as of the valuation date, adjusted for gains and losses, is often the cleanest way to divide a feature-rich defined contribution plan.

Fixed dollar award

A stated dollar amount can work, but the order should specify whether that amount shares in gains and losses after the valuation date.

Multiple money sources

If the account includes pre-tax, Roth, profit-sharing, rollover, or other sources, the order should say whether the alternate payee receives a pro rata share of each source or some other stated allocation.

Loan treatment

If there is an outstanding participant loan, the order should say whether the award is based on the gross account balance or the net balance after subtracting the loan.

Steps to Divide

  1. Confirm the exact plan name from the latest plan statement: IGNITE DIGITAL FEDERAL SERVICES LLC RETIREMENT PLAN AND TRUST.
  2. Review the current statement for all account sources, including any Roth or employer contribution buckets and any participant loan.
  3. Contact the plan administrator or benefits team to confirm current QDRO procedures and the plan's review path.
  4. Choose the valuation date and award formula.
  5. Draft explicit language for gains and losses, multiple source allocation, and loan treatment if applicable.
  6. Seek administrator pre-review before court entry where possible.
  7. Submit the signed order and required documents, then monitor implementation until the alternate payee's share is segregated or distributed.

Common Mistakes

  • Using an incomplete plan name instead of the exact Form 5500 name.
  • Drafting as if the account has only one money source when it may have several.
  • Leaving gains and losses unaddressed.
  • Ignoring participant loans.
  • Skipping administrator confirmation when no public recordkeeper was verified.
