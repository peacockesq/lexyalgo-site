In Divorce

This plan is the oddball in the batch. It is a legacy New Century Energies savings and stock ownership plan tied to a predecessor utility company that later became part of Xcel Energy. So the first divorce question is not just how to divide it, but what exactly still exists: a separate legacy plan, a frozen account, or assets already merged into a successor Xcel plan.

The Form 5500 type codes point to a defined contribution arrangement with employer-stock features. That means the order should identify the exact plan or successor plan holding the benefit, define the valuation date, address gains and losses, and state clearly how any separate sources or stock subaccounts are handled. If you skip the successor-plan check here, you are asking for an avoidable rejection.

QDRO Requirements

A qualifying domestic relations order for the NEW CENTURY ENERGIES, INC. EMPLOYEES' SAVINGS AND STOCK OWNERSHIP PLAN FOR BARGAINING UNIT EMPLOYEES AND FORMER NON-BARGAINING UNIT EMPLOYEE should be drafted to satisfy ERISA and the plan administrator's current review procedures. At minimum, the order should:

  • Name the plan exactly as shown in the plan documents and Form 5500.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with enough detail for administrator review.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula for the alternate payee award.
  • Specify the valuation date or event used for the calculation.
  • Address investment gains and losses from the valuation date through segregation or distribution.
  • Address separate contribution sources such as pre-tax, Roth, rollover, profit-sharing, or stock components if present.
  • Address participant loan treatment if a loan exists on the account.
  • Avoid awarding any benefit or form of payment the plan does not provide.
  • Because this appears to be a legacy plan, confirm whether benefits are still held under this exact plan name or under a successor Xcel Energy plan before the order goes to court.

Division Formulas

Percentage of account balance

The alternate payee receives a stated percentage of the vested account as of the valuation date, adjusted for gains and losses until segregation. This is usually the cleanest approach for a 401(k)-type plan.

Fixed dollar amount

The alternate payee receives a stated dollar amount. If you use this approach, say explicitly whether that amount is adjusted for gains and losses after the valuation date.

Source-by-source allocation

If the account includes multiple sources such as pre-tax, Roth, rollover, profit-sharing, or employer stock, the order should say whether the award is divided pro rata across each source or by another stated method.

Loan treatment

If the participant has an outstanding plan loan, the order should specify whether the alternate payee share is calculated from the gross balance, the net balance, or another agreed figure.

Steps to Divide

  1. Confirm the exact plan name from the participant's most recent statement or plan materials.
  2. Get the current account statement showing balance, contribution sources, and any outstanding loan.
  3. Before drafting, ask the participant or administrator for a current statement showing the exact plan currently holding the account.
  4. Choose the division formula: percentage award, fixed dollar award, or another clear formula.
  5. Draft explicit gains-and-losses language and source-treatment language.
  6. Send the draft for administrator pre-review before court entry when possible.
  7. After entry, submit the signed certified order and any required attachments until the alternate payee account is established.

Common Mistakes

  • Using the wrong or shortened plan name instead of the exact legal plan name.
  • Leaving out gains-and-losses language for a participant-directed account.
  • Ignoring separate contribution sources such as Roth, rollover, employer contributions, or stock funds.
  • Failing to address participant loan treatment.
  • Skipping administrator pre-review when public plan procedures are limited or unclear.
  • Drafting against the historical plan name without confirming whether the assets now sit in a successor Xcel Energy plan.

FAQ

Is this still an active standalone New Century Energies plan?

Maybe not. The research suggests this is a historical or legacy plan connected to predecessor-company retirement benefits, and public sources indicate assets may have been merged into successor Xcel Energy retirement arrangements. Confirm current status directly with Xcel Energy.

Does a QDRO still matter if the account was merged into a successor plan?

Yes. If the benefit is now held in a successor ERISA plan, the order still has to satisfy the current plan administrator's QDRO procedures and identify the correct plan holding the asset.

Why does the stock ownership feature matter?

Because the type codes indicate employer-stock or ESOP-like features. The order should make clear whether the alternate payee receives a proportionate share of each source, including any employer stock component, rather than leaving implementation to guesswork.

Who should review the draft order?

Start with Xcel Energy benefits administration or the current recordkeeper for the successor plan. For a legacy arrangement, administrator pre-review is the safest move.
