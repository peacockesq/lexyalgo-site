For QDRO purposes, the plan sponsor is Torrid LLC. 401(k) Plans in Divorce

The Torrid LLC 401(k) Plan is a defined contribution plan. Each participant holds an individual account that grows through elective deferrals, employer contributions, and investment returns. In divorce, the account balance can be divided by specifying a dollar amount, a percentage, or a formula keyed to a valuation date.

The marital portion of the account is generally the amount that accrued during the marriage. One common analytical approach is a time-based formula comparing months of marriage during plan participation to total months of participation — but that identifies what is marital property under state law; it does not itself distribute anything. The QDRO specifies the actual award, and the plan administrator implements it.

Vesting rules affect how much of the employer's contributions the participant actually owns at any given time. If the participant left Torrid before full vesting, the unvested portion of employer contributions would not be available for division. Always confirm vesting status from the participant's account statement before settling on a division figure.

QDRO Requirements

A domestic relations order for the Torrid LLC 401(k) Plan must meet ERISA requirements and the plan's qualification procedures. At minimum:

  • Identify the plan by exact name: Torrid LLC 401(K) Plan, sponsored by Torrid LLC.
  • Do not confuse this with a Hot Topic plan — they are different entities with separate plans.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with sufficient specificity for administrator review.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula for the alternate payee's share.
  • Specify the valuation date.
  • Address whether the assigned share is adjusted for investment gains and losses between the valuation date and actual segregation.
  • Confirm whether the participant has any Roth sub-account and address it if present (the Form 5500 does not indicate a Roth feature, but plan features can change; confirm from current documents).
  • Address any outstanding plan loan balance if applicable.
  • Avoid requiring any benefit not available under the plan.

Division Formulas

Percentage formula

The alternate payee receives a stated percentage of the vested account balance as of the valuation date, adjusted for gains and losses until actual segregation. This is the most common approach for defined contribution plans with identifiable balances.

Fixed dollar formula

A specific dollar award can be used when the settlement fixes a number. The order should address gains and losses after the valuation date to avoid disputes during the review and segregation period.

Gains and losses

The account balance at Torrid moves with investment markets. An order without gains/losses language creates a dispute over whether the alternate payee benefits (or suffers) from market movements during the administrator's review period. The order should be clear on this point.

Vesting

The QDRO should address how unvested employer contributions are handled. Some plans allow the alternate payee to receive only the vested portion; others vest the employer match upon QDRO approval. Confirm the plan's rules before assuming which approach applies.

Steps to Divide the Torrid LLC 401(k)

  1. Confirm the plan name and current recordkeeper with Torrid's HR or benefits department — confirm it is Torrid LLC, not a Hot Topic plan.
  2. Obtain the participant's most recent account statement showing vested and unvested balances, sub-account breakdown, and any loan information.
  3. Confirm the vesting schedule and whether any employer contributions are unvested.
  4. Determine the division method, valuation date, and gains/losses treatment.
  5. Draft the QDRO specifying the award and all relevant sub-account and gains/losses provisions.
  6. Submit for administrator pre-review before court entry if possible.
  7. After court entry, submit the certified signed order and follow through until the alternate payee's account is confirmed segregated.

Common Mistakes

  • Confusing Torrid LLC with Hot Topic: These are separate companies with separate retirement plans. A QDRO naming Hot Topic as the sponsor will not cover the Torrid 401(k).
  • Not confirming vesting: If the participant is not fully vested in employer contributions, the unvested portion is not available for division. Verify before settling on a division amount.
  • Leaving gains and losses unstated: The account moves with markets. Silence creates implementation disputes.
  • Relying on unverified contact information: John Hancock as recordkeeper comes from third-party sources. Confirm with Torrid's HR before submitting.
  • Using a recycled generic order: Boilerplate 401(k) language that omits vesting treatment, gains/losses specificity, and correct plan identity can cause rejection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Torrid LLC 401(k) require a QDRO?

Yes. An ERISA 401(k) plan cannot be divided by a divorce decree alone. A qualifying domestic relations order is required before benefits can be paid to an alternate payee.

What is Torrid LLC?

Torrid LLC is the operating entity for Torrid, a plus-size women's apparel retailer. It was spun off from Hot Topic in 2015 and went public in 2021 (NYSE: CURV). The 401(k) sponsor is Torrid LLC — not Hot Topic.

Is this the same as the Hot Topic 401(k)?

No. Torrid and Hot Topic are separate companies following the 2015 spinoff. Each has its own retirement plan. A QDRO for Torrid covers the Torrid LLC plan only.
