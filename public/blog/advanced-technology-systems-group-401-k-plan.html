401(k) in Divorce

The Advanced Technology Systems Group 401(k) Plan is a defined contribution plan, meaning the participant holds an individual account balance. In divorce, the alternate payee is typically awarded a percentage or a fixed dollar amount of that balance as of a stated valuation date. The order has to be specific enough that the administrator can act on it without guessing.

This plan includes profit sharing and Roth contribution features alongside standard 401(k) salary deferrals. If the participant has both pre-tax and Roth money in the account, the order should address both sources. The auto-enrollment feature is informational context; the key drafting issues remain the same: valuation date, gains and losses, source allocation, and loan treatment if applicable.

QDRO Requirements

A qualifying domestic relations order for the Advanced Technology Systems Group 401(k) Plan must satisfy ERISA's minimum content requirements and whatever procedures the plan administrator applies. At minimum, the order should:

  • Name the plan exactly: ADVANCED TECHNOLOGY SYSTEMS GROUP 401(K) PLAN.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee clearly enough for administrator review.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula used to determine the alternate payee's share.
  • Specify the valuation date or event controlling the calculation.
  • Address gains and losses from the valuation date through segregation or distribution.
  • Address pre-tax and Roth source treatment if both are present in the account.
  • Address participant loan treatment if a loan exists.
  • Avoid requiring any benefit form or option the plan does not provide.

Division Formulas

Percentage award

The alternate payee receives a stated percentage of the participant's vested account balance as of the valuation date, adjusted for gains and losses through segregation. This tracks the account naturally and is the most common approach for 401(k) plans.

Fixed dollar amount

The alternate payee receives a specific dollar figure. The order should address whether this amount adjusts for investment gains and losses after the valuation date; silence on that point creates avoidable disputes.

Pre-tax and Roth sources

If the participant holds both pre-tax and Roth money, specify how each source is allocated. A pro rata division — the same percentage of each source as the overall award — is usually the cleanest drafting choice.

Loan balance treatment

If there is an outstanding participant loan, state whether the award is calculated from the gross balance before the loan or the net balance after. That decision affects the alternate payee's actual receive amount.

Steps to Divide

  1. Confirm the exact plan name from the participant's benefit statement: ADVANCED TECHNOLOGY SYSTEMS GROUP 401(K) PLAN.
  2. Obtain the most recent account statement showing total balance, source balances (pre-tax, Roth, employer), and any outstanding loan balance.
  3. Contact the plan sponsor to confirm the current recordkeeper and QDRO review procedures.
  4. Choose the division method: percentage, fixed dollar amount, or another clearly stated formula.
  5. Draft with explicit gains and losses, source allocation, and loan treatment language.
  6. Submit for administrator pre-review before court entry when possible.
  7. After entry, submit the certified signed order and follow through until the alternate payee account is established.

Common Mistakes

  • Wrong plan name: using shorthand instead of the exact Form 5500 plan name.
  • No gains and losses language: the account keeps moving after the valuation date; the order should say what happens to the alternate payee's share.
  • Ignoring Roth balances: if the participant has Roth contributions, the order should address that source explicitly.
  • Overlooking the loan: an outstanding 401(k) loan changes the economics of the award.
  • Assuming the recordkeeper without confirming: third-party directory data can be stale; confirm with the sponsor before submitting.

FAQ

Does dividing this plan in divorce require a QDRO?

Yes. A divorce decree alone cannot divide an ERISA 401(k) plan. A qualified domestic relations order meeting federal requirements and the plan's procedures is required before any alternate payee distribution.

Who processes QDROs for this plan?

Third-party sources suggest American Funds, but that was not confirmed from an official plan document. Contact the plan sponsor directly to get the current recordkeeper and QDRO submission procedures.

Should the order address both Roth and pre-tax balances?

Yes, if both exist. State how each source is divided to prevent the administrator from having to guess about source allocation.

Why does gains and losses language matter?

Because this is a participant-directed account. The balance changes after the valuation date; the order should specify whether the alternate payee's share tracks investment performance through segregation.
