Dividing the Amazon 401(k) in Divorce

The Amazon 401(k) Plan is a defined contribution plan — the participant holds an individual account reflecting their own deferrals, any employer matching contributions, and investment returns over time. In divorce, the account is typically divided by specifying a percentage of the participant's vested balance or a fixed dollar amount, measured as of a valuation date agreed upon by the parties or set by court order.

Because account values fluctuate with investment performance, the order should address whether the alternate payee's share is adjusted for gains and losses between the valuation date and the date of segregation. Without that clause, the result depends on how the administrator interprets silence — which varies by plan.

No coverture formula: The Amazon 401(k) Plan is a defined contribution plan and does not use the marital coverture formula as a distribution method. The marital fraction is an analysis tool, not an award structure. The QDRO must express the alternate payee's share as a dollar amount or percentage of the account balance as of a specified date.

QDRO Requirements

A domestic relations order for the Amazon 401(k) Plan must satisfy ERISA's qualification requirements and the plan's own review procedures. At minimum, the order should:

  • Identify the plan by name: Amazon 401(k) Plan.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with sufficient specificity for Fidelity's records.
  • State the award as a specific percentage or dollar amount — not a coverture fraction or marital share formula.
  • Identify the valuation date or triggering event from which the balance is measured.
  • State how investment gains and losses are handled from the valuation date through actual segregation.
  • Address both pre-tax and Roth contribution sources if the participant holds both.
  • Avoid requiring any benefit not available under the plan.
  • Comply with any current Fidelity or Amazon-specific submission and format requirements.

Division Formulas

Percentage of vested balance

Award the alternate payee a set percentage — typically 50% — of the participant's vested account balance as of the valuation date. If the settlement includes a gains-and-losses clause, the share tracks investment performance through segregation. Clear, common, and straightforward to administer.

Fixed dollar amount

Where the parties agree on a hard number, the order should state whether that amount earns at plan investment rates from the valuation date through segregation, or whether it is a frozen figure. A frozen fixed amount can lead to unexpected shortfalls or surpluses depending on market movement before segregation.

Pre-tax and Roth treatment

If the participant's account includes both pre-tax and Roth sources, the order should state how the award is allocated across those sources. A simple pro rata approach allocates the same percentage from each tax bucket. Without explicit treatment, the administrator's default method may not match the parties' intent.

Gains and losses

The most common drafting gap in 401(k) QDROs. The order should clearly state that the alternate payee's share is adjusted for all investment experience — gains and losses — from the valuation date through the date the account is actually segregated. This avoids post-qualification disputes when market conditions change.

Steps to Divide the Amazon 401(k)

  1. Obtain the participant's current Fidelity account statement showing total balance, pre-tax and Roth sub-balances, vesting status, and any outstanding loans.
  2. Contact Fidelity's QDRO Center or the 401(k) Committee (206-266-1000) to confirm current QDRO procedures, submission channel, and any required cover documentation.
  3. Request the current Amazon-specific QDRO requirements or model order language from Fidelity before drafting.
  4. Draft the order specifying the award formula, valuation date, gains/losses clause, and pre-tax/Roth treatment.
  5. Submit the draft for pre-qualification review if the plan allows it, before presenting the order to the court.
  6. After court entry, submit the certified signed order through the confirmed submission channel (Fidelity QDRO Center or as otherwise directed).
  7. Follow through until the plan issues a written qualification letter and the alternate payee's segregated account is confirmed.

Common Mistakes

  • Using a coverture formula: Defined contribution plans like the Amazon 401(k) do not accept the coverture formula as an award method. Express the share as a dollar amount or percentage.
  • Omitting gains and losses language: Without a gains-and-losses clause, parties may dispute whether the alternate payee's share should reflect investment performance between the valuation date and segregation.
  • Missing Roth treatment: If the account has both pre-tax and Roth contributions, leaving the allocation between sources unstated can complicate segregation and create tax ambiguity.
  • Bypassing Fidelity's QDRO Center: For plans under Fidelity administration, using a generic mailing address rather than the dedicated QDRO submission channel can delay qualification review.
  • Skipping pre-approval review: Submitting a draft for review before court entry, where the plan allows it, reduces the risk of rejection after the order is already signed and entered.

FAQ

Does the Amazon 401(k) Plan require a QDRO?

Yes. A divorce judgment alone does not divide an ERISA plan. The Amazon 401(k) requires a domestic relations order that satisfies ERISA and the plan's qualification requirements.

Who administers the Amazon 401(k) Plan?

The DOL Form 5500 identifies the 401(K) Committee at 440 Terry Avenue North, Seattle, WA 98109 (206-266-1000) as the plan administrator. Fidelity is the recordkeeper and handles QDRO review through its QDRO Center.

Can I use a coverture fraction for the Amazon 401(k)?

No. The award must be stated as a specific dollar amount or percentage of the vested account balance as of a stated valuation date. Coverture fractions are not accepted as distribution formulas by defined contribution plans.

How do I submit a QDRO for the Amazon 401(k)?

Submit through Fidelity's QDRO Center, which is the known official channel for Amazon 401(k) QDRO qualification. Confirm the current submission process and required documents directly with Fidelity before filing.

Was an official model QDRO found for the Amazon 401(k)?

No. No official public model QDRO was identified in this research. Contact Fidelity's QDRO Center or the 401(k) Committee for current model order language and plan-specific procedures.
