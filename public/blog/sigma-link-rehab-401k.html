How a 401(k) Works in Divorce

The Sigma Link Rehab 401(k) Plan is a defined contribution plan. That means each participant has an individual account balance — not a formula-based pension. In divorce, the account can typically be divided by a percentage of the balance as of a specific date, a fixed dollar amount, or a formula that ties the award to the marital period of contributions.

Because the account is invested and its value changes daily, the order needs to be precise about timing. A gain or loss between the date used for division and the date the alternate payee's share is actually segregated can materially affect the final amount. The order should say clearly whether the alternate payee's share tracks that market movement or is frozen at the valuation date.

Form 5500 data indicates this plan includes both employer matching contributions and profit sharing, as well as elective participant deferrals. Depending on the vesting schedule and the parties' agreement, the QDRO may need to address multiple account sources — not just the participant's own contributions.

QDRO Requirements

A well-drafted domestic relations order for the Sigma Link Rehab 401(k) Plan should address:

  • Identification of the correct plan by exact name: Sigma Link Rehab 401(K) Plan.

  • The exact amount, percentage, or formula determining the alternate payee's share.
  • The valuation date used to calculate the award.
  • Whether the assigned share is adjusted for investment gains and losses between the valuation date and the date of actual segregation or distribution.
  • Which account sources (elective deferrals, employer match, profit sharing) are included in the award.
  • Any outstanding loan balance, if applicable, and how it affects the calculation.

Division Formulas

Percentage of balance

Award the alternate payee a percentage of the participant's vested account balance as of a specified date, adjusted for subsequent investment gains and losses through the date of segregation. This is a clean approach for plans with daily valuation — the segregated amount reflects the same performance as the underlying account.

Fixed dollar amount

A fixed dollar award specifies a set dollar figure from the participant's account. The order should still address gains and losses — if the account value drops after the valuation date, a frozen fixed-dollar award may exceed the available balance. Clarity on this point prevents processing disputes.

Source-specific allocation

Where the account includes deferrals, employer match, and profit sharing, the order can specify each source pro rata or by a different method. Spelling this out prevents ambiguity when the recordkeeper processes the split, especially if some employer contributions are unvested at the time of division.

Gains and losses language

With daily valuation, a gains-and-losses clause is straightforward: the alternate payee's assigned share should grow or shrink with the account's investment experience from the valuation date through segregation. Without this, the award is effectively frozen and the parties may dispute the difference.

Common Mistakes

  • Using a generic order: boilerplate language built for a large recordkeeper may not map cleanly to Sigma Link Rehab's plan structure. Draft specifically for this plan.
  • Leaving gains and losses silent: for a daily-valued account, failing to address this creates a real dollar ambiguity. State it clearly.
  • Ignoring employer contributions: if the plan has both match and profit sharing, the award may need to cover all sources, not just elective deferrals. Confirm the vesting schedule first.
  • Skipping pre-submission review: for small plans without published procedures, sending a cold order to the administrator without prior contact can lead to rejection or unnecessary delay.
  • Missing the correct plan name: use the exact plan name — Sigma Link Rehab 401(K) Plan — to avoid any question about which plan is targeted.

FAQ

Does the Sigma Link Rehab 401(k) Plan require a QDRO for divorce division?

Yes. An ERISA-covered 401(k) plan requires a qualifying domestic relations order before plan assets can be paid to an alternate payee. A divorce judgment alone does not transfer retirement account rights.

What type of plan is this?

It is a participant-directed 401(k) defined contribution plan with individual accounts, employer matching contributions, and profit sharing. The plan uses daily valuation. Each participant's account reflects their own contributions, employer contributions, and investment experience over time.

Who do I contact for QDRO procedures?

Contact Sigma Link Rehab, LLC's HR department at 1413 38th Street, Brooklyn, NY. No separate third-party administrator was identified in public research. The participant's most recent account statement may include contact information for the current recordkeeper.

Is there a model QDRO for this plan?

No official model QDRO was found in public research. For small plans like this one, direct communication with the plan administrator before drafting is especially important — procedures vary and a cold submission without pre-review often leads to avoidable rejection or amendment.

Do gains and losses matter for this plan?

Yes. Because this is a daily-valued defined contribution account, the balance changes with investment performance. The order should state whether the alternate payee's share is adjusted for gains and losses between the valuation date and the date the account is actually divided. Silence on this point is a common source of post-entry disputes.

Does the employer match have to be included in the award?

Not necessarily — it depends on the vesting schedule and the parties' agreement. If employer match contributions are partially or fully unvested at the time of division, the order needs to address whether the alternate payee receives only the vested portion or whether a formula accounts for future vesting. Confirm the vesting schedule with the plan administrator.
