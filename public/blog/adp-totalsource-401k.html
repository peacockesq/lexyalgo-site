401(k) in Divorce

The ADP TotalSource Retirement Savings Plan is a defined contribution arrangement, which means the number that matters most is the participant's account balance rather than a future monthly pension formula. In divorce, that usually makes the core drafting question more concrete: what share of the account is being awarded, on what date, and how should gains, losses, and fees be treated after that date?

For plans like this one, a divorce judgment by itself generally does not move money to a former spouse. The plan administrator typically needs a qualified domestic relations order that satisfies ERISA, the Internal Revenue Code, and the plan's own procedures before a separate account can be established for the alternate payee.

Because ADP is a broad corporate label, precision matters. The order should target the ADP TotalSource Retirement Savings Plan specifically, not just "ADP" or a vague reference to a 401(k), so there is no confusion about which plan is being divided.

QDRO Requirements

A usable order for this plan should do the administrative basics cleanly and leave as little room for interpretation as possible. That usually means the document should:

  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with enough detail for plan administration.
  • Name the ADP TotalSource Retirement Savings Plan exactly and consistently throughout the order.
  • State the award as a dollar amount or percentage tied to a specific valuation date.
  • Explain whether post-valuation gains or losses are included on the awarded share.
  • Address how outstanding loans, if any, are treated in the valuation.
  • Stay within plan terms and avoid asking the administrator to create a benefit form the plan does not offer.
Drafting note: Where plan-specific review is available, sending a draft for pre-approval before court entry can save time and reduce rejection risk.

Division Formulas

For the ADP TotalSource Retirement Savings Plan, the safest drafting approach is usually straightforward math. Instead of using an abstract marital-service formula, the order should define the alternate payee award in a format the administrator can calculate directly from the account.

  • Percentage approach: Award the alternate payee a stated percentage of the participant's vested account balance as of a defined valuation date.
  • Dollar approach: Award the alternate payee a fixed dollar amount as of a defined valuation date.
  • Investment adjustment: Specify whether the awarded share is adjusted for gains and losses from the valuation date to segregation or distribution.
  • Source treatment: If relevant, clarify whether the award is allocated proportionately across plan sources and investment holdings.

The cleaner the valuation language, the better. Defined contribution plan administrators usually want language they can apply directly to account records without reconstructing the marriage timeline from outside evidence.

Steps

  1. Confirm the exact plan name and current QDRO submission procedures for the ADP TotalSource Retirement Savings Plan.
  2. Obtain the relevant account statement or valuation support for the agreed date.
  3. Draft the order using a clear dollar or percentage award and explicit valuation-date language.
  4. Address gains, losses, fees, and any loan treatment so the economics are predictable.
  5. Submit a draft for plan-side review if available before seeking final court signature.
  6. After entry, submit the signed order promptly and follow through until the administrator confirms qualification and segregation.

Common Mistakes

  • Referring only to "ADP" instead of the full ADP TotalSource Retirement Savings Plan name.
  • Using an undefined or poorly documented valuation date.
  • Failing to state whether gains and losses apply after the valuation date.
  • Ignoring the possibility that QDRO processing charges may be assessed to the account.
  • Submitting the order to the court first without checking whether draft review is available.
  • Using language that requires the administrator to infer intent rather than calculate a concrete award.

FAQ

Why does the order need to identify the ADP TotalSource plan specifically?

Because "ADP" can refer to multiple retirement arrangements or related entities. Precise identification reduces the chance of rejection, delay, or confusion about which plan is being divided.

Who administers the ADP TotalSource Retirement Savings Plan for QDRO purposes?

The research used for this page indicates Voya is involved in plan administration. Before drafting or filing, confirm the current submission address, review procedures, and contact details for this exact plan.

How should the alternate payee award usually be stated?

Most often as a specific dollar amount or percentage of the participant account as of a defined valuation date, with clear language about gains and losses after that date.

Can the plan charge fees related to QDRO processing?

Yes. The referenced SPD indicates QDRO processing costs may be charged to participant accounts, so parties should account for that possibility in settlement planning.

Should the order be submitted for draft review before court entry?

Usually yes. Draft review can help surface plan-specific issues before the order is signed by the judge, which often saves time and reduces revision cycles.
