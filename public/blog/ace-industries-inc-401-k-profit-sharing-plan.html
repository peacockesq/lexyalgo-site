401(k) Profit Sharing Plan in Divorce

Ace Industries' plan is a defined contribution plan — the participant holds an individual account rather than a pension formula. The profit sharing component means employer contributions are likely made on a discretionary basis, which may be tied to company performance. Those contributions typically vest over time, so the participant's account may include unvested employer balances depending on their tenure.

Because FuturePlan acts as a 3(16) fiduciary administrator rather than just a recordkeeper, they have direct authority over QDRO qualification decisions. Submitting a draft to FuturePlan for pre-review before court entry is particularly important here — you are dealing with an entity that holds fiduciary responsibility, not just an administrative clearinghouse.

QDRO Requirements

A qualifying domestic relations order for the Ace Industries 401(k) Profit Sharing Plan must satisfy ERISA's minimum content requirements and FuturePlan's review procedures. The order should:

  • Name the plan exactly: Ace Industries, Inc. 401(k) Profit Sharing Plan.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with sufficient specificity for FuturePlan's review.
  • State the dollar amount, percentage, or formula used to determine the alternate payee's share.
  • Specify the valuation date or triggering event for the calculation.
  • Address investment gains and losses from the valuation date through segregation or distribution.
  • Address vesting — specify whether the award covers only the vested balance or includes a provision for unvested amounts that vest later.
  • Avoid requiring any type or form of benefit not available under the plan.

Division Formulas

Percentage of vested account

The alternate payee receives a stated percentage of the participant's total vested account balance as of the valuation date, adjusted for investment gains and losses until segregation. This covers both 401(k) deferrals and profit sharing contributions proportionally.

Fixed dollar amount

A specific dollar amount can work when the settlement calls for a precise number. Address whether gains and losses apply between the valuation date and segregation — a frozen fixed amount may not reflect the account's actual value by the time distribution occurs.

Vesting and profit sharing

If the participant has unvested profit sharing balances, the order should either limit the award to the currently vested balance or specify how unvested amounts will be treated when they vest. The cleaner approach is usually to limit the division to vested amounts as of the valuation date and state that explicitly.

Gains and losses

Participant-directed accounts change with investment performance. The order should say whether the alternate payee's assigned share tracks those changes from the valuation date through the date of segregation, or whether the award is fixed at the valuation date amount.

Common Mistakes

  • Sending the order to the wrong entity: because FuturePlan is the named fiduciary administrator, QDRO submissions should go through FuturePlan — not just to Ace Industries HR alone.
  • Ignoring unvested profit sharing: if the participant holds unvested employer contributions, the order should address whether and how those amounts are included. Silence on vesting leaves the alternate payee's interest on those balances unresolved.
  • No gains and losses language: defined contribution accounts fluctuate with market performance. Without explicit language on gains and losses, the award value is uncertain between the valuation date and segregation.
  • Wrong plan name: using a shortened name instead of Ace Industries, Inc. 401(k) Profit Sharing Plan can cause a rejection at the administrator level.
  • Skipping pre-review: given FuturePlan's direct fiduciary authority over QDRO qualification, a pre-review draft submission is worth the extra step.

FAQ

Does dividing the Ace Industries 401(k) require a QDRO?

Yes. This is an ERISA-governed retirement plan. A divorce judgment alone cannot direct it to pay benefits to an alternate payee. A qualifying domestic relations order is required.

Who is the plan administrator?

FuturePlan Fiduciary Services (an Ascensus company) is named directly in the Form 5500 as the plan administrator. FuturePlan holds 3(16) fiduciary responsibility, which means they have direct authority over QDRO qualification decisions.

What is FuturePlan's role compared to a typical recordkeeper?

A recordkeeper typically handles account tracking and transactions. A 3(16) fiduciary administrator like FuturePlan goes further — they take on legal and fiduciary responsibility for plan administration, including determining whether an order qualifies as a QDRO. This is a more authoritative role than a standard recordkeeper.

Should unvested profit sharing balances be addressed?

Yes, if they are relevant to the settlement. The safest approach is to limit the division to the vested balance as of the valuation date and state that clearly. If the parties want to capture future vesting, that provision needs to be spelled out explicitly.
