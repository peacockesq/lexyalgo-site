About the Plan

The Deferred Profit-Sharing Plan for Salaried Employees covers salaried employees of Altria Client Services LLC and affiliated operating companies. Altria's primary operating subsidiaries include Philip Morris USA (cigarettes and tobacco products), U.S. Smokeless Tobacco Company, and Middleton (cigars). The company is headquartered in Richmond, Virginia.

Despite being titled as a "Deferred Profit-Sharing Plan," the Form 5500 benefit type codes confirm the plan includes: a 401(k) elective deferral feature (code 2E), profit-sharing contributions (code 2F), Roth 401(k) contributions (code 2S), and an ESOP component (code 2O). The ESOP feature means participants may hold Altria Group Inc. (MO) company stock within the plan. Company stock in an ESOP component requires specific attention in QDRO drafting because its handling — including whether it is converted to cash, transferred in-kind, or handled differently — may be governed by plan-specific rules.

The large decrease from 9,365 beginning-of-year participants to 4,168 active participants likely reflects Altria's significant workforce reductions and restructuring as tobacco sales have declined and the company has shifted strategy toward smokeless products and cannabis investments.

Defined Contribution Plans in Divorce

As a defined contribution plan, the Deferred Profit-Sharing Plan holds an individual account for each participant. The account balance at any point reflects cumulative contributions plus or minus investment returns, less any loans or withdrawals. In divorce, this balance can be divided by specifying a percentage, dollar amount, or formula keyed to a valuation date.

The marital portion of the account is the amount that accrued during the marriage. A time-based analysis comparing months of marriage during plan participation to total participation months is one analytical approach to identify the marital share — but that calculation identifies what is marital, it does not itself constitute the QDRO award. The QDRO specifies the actual award; the plan administrator then implements it.

QDRO Requirements

A domestic relations order for this plan must meet ERISA requirements and the plan's qualification procedures. At minimum:

  • Identify the plan by exact name: Deferred Profit-Sharing Plan for Salaried Employees, sponsored by Altria Client Services LLC.
  • Identify the participant and alternate payee with sufficient specificity.
  • State the amount, percentage, or formula for the alternate payee's share.
  • Specify the valuation date.
  • Address investment gains and losses between the valuation date and actual segregation.
  • Address Roth and pre-tax sub-accounts if the participant has both.
  • Address any ESOP (company stock) holdings — including whether stock is transferred in-kind or liquidated for the alternate payee's share.
  • Address any outstanding plan loan balance if applicable.
  • Avoid requiring any benefit not available under the plan.

Division Formulas

Percentage formula

The alternate payee receives a stated percentage of the vested account balance as of the valuation date, adjusted for gains and losses until segregation. This is the most common and administratively clean approach for defined contribution accounts.

Fixed dollar formula

A specific dollar amount can be awarded. The QDRO should address whether the dollar figure is adjusted for investment experience after the valuation date.

ESOP / company stock handling

The ESOP feature means the participant may hold Altria (MO) stock within the plan. The QDRO should say whether the alternate payee's proportionate share of company stock is included in the division, and whether it is transferred in-kind or converted to cash upon segregation. This should be confirmed against the plan's specific procedures.

Roth and pre-tax sources

If the participant holds both Roth and pre-tax balances, a pro-rata allocation across sources — dividing each sub-account proportionally in the same ratio as the overall award — provides clear, implementable language without leaving source-level treatment ambiguous.

Common Mistakes

  • Missing the ESOP component: A generic 401(k) QDRO template may not address company stock holdings. If ESOP stock is present, the order needs explicit treatment.
  • Ignoring Roth sub-accounts: The plan includes Roth 401(k) features. If both pre-tax and Roth balances exist, each source should be addressed.
  • Using the wrong entity name: The sponsor is Altria Client Services LLC, not Philip Morris USA or Altria Group Inc. directly. The plan name is "Deferred Profit-Sharing Plan for Salaried Employees."
  • Skipping gains and losses language: Investment account balances move during the review period. The order should say whether the alternate payee's share is adjusted.
  • Not verifying the current recordkeeper: Fidelity is identified only by third-party sources. The named Form 5500 administrator (Thomas H. Watson) and the Altria Group Benefits Center are official touchpoints that should be used to confirm current QDRO procedures.
