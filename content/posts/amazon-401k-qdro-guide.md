# How to Divide the Amazon 401(k) Plan in Divorce — A QDRO Guide

### What the Amazon 401(k) Plan Covers

The plan allows eligible employees to contribute:

- **Pre-tax deferrals** — contributions reduce taxable income for the year
- **Roth (after-tax) deferrals** — contributions taxed now, distributions tax-free in retirement
- **Employer match** — Amazon may provide matching contributions (details vary by employee group and year)

Both the employee's contributions and any employer match are subject to division in divorce, subject to vesting rules and state law.

## How 401(k) Plans Are Treated in Divorce

### The Marital Portion

The portion of the Amazon 401(K) Plan that accrued **during the marriage** may be subject to division in divorce.

This depends heavily on state law and the specific plan terms. The actual division of a 401(k) in divorce is done through a Qualified Domestic Relations Order (QDRO), which specifies the amount or percentage awarded to the alternate payee. The QDRO itself simply states what the alternate payee gets, without prescribing how that number was calculated.

**Separate property tracing:** Depending on state law, premarital contributions and their growth may be separate property. Post-marital contributions are typically marital. State law varies significantly on how growth attributable to separate property during marriage is treated.

### Qualified vs. Non-Qualified

A 401(k) is a qualified retirement plan under ERISA. This means it **must** be divided using a Qualified Domestic Relations Order (QDRO) — not a regular court order. The QDRO is reviewed and approved by the plan administrator before any distribution can occur.

## QDRO Requirements for the Amazon 401(k) Plan

### Plan Administrator Contact

When preparing a QDRO for the Amazon 401(K) Plan, the plan administrator to contact is:

- **401(K) Committee**, c/o Amazon.com Services, LLC, Seattle, WA

Always verify the current administrator address and required QDRO procedures directly with Amazon's HR or benefits department. Plan procedures can change, and outdated information can lead to QDRO rejection.

### Key Rules That Affect QDRO Drafting

Based on typical 401(k) plan requirements:

- The QDRO must clearly identify:
  - The participant's full legal name
  - The alternate payee's full legal name and contact information
  - The amount or percentage of the benefit to be awarded
  - The number of payments or time period to which the order applies
- The QDRO must **not** require the plan to provide any benefit not otherwise available under the plan
- Loans, if outstanding, must be addressed in the QDRO (the alternate payee may receive a reduced share if a loan is outstanding)
- Employer match vesting rules may affect how much of the employer-contributed portion is actually divisible
- If records are lost or the plan administrator cannot run the calculations for some reason, the parties may need to do their own calculations

## Division Formulas for 401(k) Plans

### How the Award Is Specified

The QDRO specifies the alternate payee's share using one of these approaches:

1. **Percentage-Based Award** — Straight percentage of the account
   - "Alternate payee shall receive 50% of the participant's vested account balance as of the date of separation"

2. **Fixed Dollar Award** — Specific dollar amount
   - Less common for 401(k) plans because it doesn't account for market fluctuations

**Note:** The actual calculation of the award is done by the plan administrator using the percentage or fixed amount specified in the QDRO.

### Handling Gains and Losses

Between the date of separation and QDRO approval, the account may gain or lose value. The QDRO should specify:

- Whether the alternate payee's share includes gains/losses during the QDRO review period
- The valuation date to be used

### Tax Treatment

**Important:** QDRO-approved distributions from a 401(k) plan to an alternate payee are **not** subject to the 10% early withdrawal penalty that applies to non-QDRO 401(k) withdrawals before age 59½. However, distributions are still taxed as ordinary income unless rolled over into an IRA or another qualified plan.

## Steps to Divide the Amazon 401(k) Plan

1. **Request plan documents** — Get a current summary plan description and account statement from Amazon's benefits department

2. **Calculate your share** — Use the percentage or fixed dollar award specified in your divorce decree or settlement agreement

3. **Draft the QDRO** — Ensure it meets all ERISA and plan-specific requirements

4. **Submit to the court** — For review and signature

5. **Submit the approved QDRO to the plan administrator** — Amazon's 401(K) Committee reviews for QDRO compliance

6. **Wait for determination** — The plan administrator will approve, request revision, or reject the QDRO

7. **Distribution or rollover** — Once approved, the alternate payee can typically roll over the distributed amount into an IRA

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Waiting Until After the Divorce

Many attorneys leave QDROs until after the divorce is filed. During the divorce, your accounts are protected by automatic temporary restraining orders (ATROs). Once the divorce judgment is entered, those protections disappear.

### Not Freezing the Accounts

A simple Notice of Adverse Interest to the plan administrator can freeze the account while the QDRO is being prepared. Many people skip this step and regret it later.

### Failing to Trace Properly

If the participant contributed before marriage, those premarital contributions and their growth may be separate property under state law. Proper tracing is essential — don't assume.

### Ignoring Outstanding Loans

If the participant has an outstanding loan from the plan, it affects the divisible balance. The QDRO must address this.

### Not Specifying Gains/Losses

Who bears the market change during QDRO review? This must be addressed in the order.
