Dividing property is one of the most stressful parts of a divorce because people are often comparing assets that look equal on paper but behave very differently in real life.

Start by separating marital property from separate property. That line matters more than most people realize, and it should be documented early.

Then look at the rules in your state. Some states use community property rules and others use equitable distribution, which means the analysis can shift a lot even when the facts look similar.

Finally, do not negotiate from a spreadsheet that ignores taxes, liquidity, or timing. A dollar in a retirement account is not the same as a dollar in cash, and the wrong trade can follow you for years.
